## Lesson 36: To Look Like: 보이다 / ~아/어 보이다

### Key Grammar Points

#### 보이다 — To Be Seen / To See
Passive form of **보다** (to see). Used to say something can/cannot be seen.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 저는 소방서가 **보여요** | I can see the fire station |
| TV가 **안 보여** | I can't see the TV |
| 가격표가 **안 보여요** | I can't see the price tags |

> **Note:** Korean uses an intransitive verb (보이다), but English translates it as transitive ("I can't see"). Literally: "The TV can't be seen."

---

#### ~ 같이 보이다 — To Look Like (a noun)
Place **같이** after a noun and predicate with **보이다**:

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 너는 원숭이 **같이 보여** | You look like a monkey |
| 저의 남자친구는 교수님 **같이 보여요** | My boyfriend looks like the professor |
| 너는 고등학생 **같이 보여** | You look like a high school student |

**With ~는 것:**
- 네가 새로운 집으로 이사하고 싶은 것 **같이 보여** = It looks like you want to move

---

#### Noun + 맛/냄새 같다 — To Taste/Smell Like

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 김치 **맛 같아** | Tastes like kimchi |
| 삼겹살은 베이컨 **맛 같아요** | 삼겹살 tastes like bacon |
| 김치 찌개 **냄새 같아** | It smells like kimchi jjigae |

---

#### ~아/어 보이다 — To Look (Adjective)
Add ~아/어 to an adjective + **보aida** to say someone/something looks [adjective]:

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 왜 그렇게 **행복해 보여요**? | Why do you look so happy? |
| 그 남자 종아리가 아주 **강해 보여요** | That man's calves look very strong |
| 무릎이 **아파 보여요** | Your knee looks sore |
| 소방 훈련이 **힘들어 보여요** | Firefighting training looks difficult |
| 화장이 **예뻐 보여요** | Your makeup looks pretty |
| 그 남자가 **의욕이 많아 보여요** | That man looks like he has a lot of drive |

---

#### 들리다 — To Be Heard
Passive form of **듣다** (to hear):

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 소리가 **안 들려요** | I can't hear anything |
| 목소리가 **잘 들려** | I hear your voice well |
| 뭔가 터지는 소리가 **들렸어요** | I heard something explode |
