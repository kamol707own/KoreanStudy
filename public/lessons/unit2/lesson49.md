## Key Grammar

### Another meaning of 되다 — "No problem" / "Works well"

되다 can indicate something is going well or working:

> **사업이 잘 되고 있어요?** = Is your business going well?

> **여기서 Wi-Fi가 잘 돼요** = The Wi-Fi here works well

> **밥이 됐어요?** = Is the rice ready?

Use **안** (not 못) to negate: **컴퓨터가 안 돼요** = The computer isn't working

---

### ~아/어도 되다 — "You may…" / "It's okay to…"

Attach ~아/어도 to a verb stem, then add 되다 to say there is no problem with the action. Translates to "you may" or "you are allowed to."

**Statements:**

> **지금 문을 열어도 돼요** = You may open the door now

> **제일 편리한 것을 선택해도 돼요** = You may choose the most convenient one

> **당구를 여기서 쳐도 돼요** = You are allowed to play pool here

> **이 직장에서 수염을 길러도 돼요** = You are allowed to have a beard at this job

**Questions** ("May I…?" / "Is it alright if…?"):

> **화장실에 가도 돼요?** = May I go to the bathroom?

> **얼룩말에게 먹이를 줘도 돼요?** = May I give feed to the zebras?

> **죄수를 보러 교도소에 들어가도 돼요?** = May we go into the prison to see the prisoners?

**Past/future tense of 되다:**

> **그때 나가도 되었어요** = It would have been okay if you left at that time

> **문을 열어도 될 거예요** = It will be okay if you open the door

---

### 안 ~아/어도 되다 — "You don't have to…"

Negate the verb before ~아/어도 with 안 or ~지 않다, then add 되다. Translates to "one doesn't have to" or "it's okay if one doesn't."

> **내일까지 다 안 해도 돼요** = You don't need to do it all by tomorrow

> **여기에 사인을 안 해도 돼요** = You don't need to sign here

> **수염을 안 깎아도 돼요?** = You don't need to shave your beard?

> **핸드폰에 연락처 기능이 있어서 요즘에 사람들은 기억력이 좋지 않아도 돼요** = Cell phones have contact functions so these days people don't need to have a good memory

This is the opposite of ~아/어야 하다 (Lesson 46):

- **공부를 안 해도 돼요** = I don't need to study
- **공부를 해야 돼요** = I need to study

---

### 좋다 / 괜찮다 as alternatives to 되다

Replace 되다 with 좋다 or 괜찮다 for the same "it's okay" meaning:

> **지금 문을 열어도 돼요 / 좋아요 / 괜찮아요** = You may open the door now (all three are equivalent)

> **밥을 많이 먹어도 돼요? / 좋아요? / 괜찮아요?** = May I eat a lot?
