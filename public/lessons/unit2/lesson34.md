## Lesson 34: Explanations for Difficult Words

### Key Grammar Points

#### ~거리다 (Repeated Action Suffix)
Added to certain verbs to indicate a repeated or ongoing action, often with a descriptive/feeling nuance.

- **흔들다** (to shake) → **흔들거리다** (to be shaking/wobbly)
- **머뭇거리다** (to hesitate) — no base form "머뭇다"
- **반짝거리다** (to be shiny/sparkly) — use **반짝반짝** for "bling-bling"
- **두리번거리다** (to look around) — no base form "두리번다"
- **끈적거리다** (to be sticky)
- **출렁거리다** (to be sloshing/rocking)

> **Note:** Only certain words can take ~거리다. Don't attach it to random words.

---

#### 자기 vs 자신
- **자기**: Used when a person has already been mentioned; replaces "he/she/that person" the second time. Also used by married couples (e.g., **자기야!**).
- **자신**: Used when the person is both the subject AND object (oneself). Can also mean "confidence" (자신감).

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 그는 **자기** 외모를 좋아해요 | He likes his (own) appearance |
| 저는 **제 자신**을 안 믿어요 | I don't trust myself |

---

#### Still — 아직 / 여전히
| Form | Nuance |
|------|--------|
| **아직** | Still/yet (present tense: still; past negative: yet) |
| **아직도** | Still (emphasizing it shouldn't be the case anymore) |
| **아직은** | Still (but will change soon) |
| **여전히** | Still (and will continue into foreseeable future) |

---

#### Words Requiring Negative Endings
| Word | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **별로** | not very/really | 밥은 맛있어? — **별로야** (Meh, not really) |
| **전혀** | not at all | 저는 **전혀** 배고프지 않아 (I'm not hungry at all) |
| **하나도** | not even one/not at all | 저는 친구가 **하나도** 없어요 (I don't have even one friend) |

---

#### 훨씬 = Much More
Used like **더** but stronger.
- 한국어는 영어보다 **훨씬** 어렵다 = Korean is much more difficult than English
- **훨씬 더** 똑똑해 = way/much smarter

---

#### 관하다 / 관련하다 = Regarding/Related to
~에 **관하다** / ~에 **관련하다** — similar to ~에 대하다 (about).

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 한국역사에 **관한** 영화 | a movie related to Korean history |
| 환경에 **관련된** 영화 | a movie relating to the environment |

---

#### 주위 = Around/Surroundings
- Physical: **학교 주위를 걷다** (to walk around the school)
- Area: **주위가 안전하지 않아요** (The surroundings aren't safe)
- People: **주위 사람들** (people around one — friends, family, coworkers)

---

#### 일단 = Once/First/For now
| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 일단 제가 밥을 먹은 후에 밖에 나갈 거예요 | Once I eat, I will go outside |
| 일단 숙제를 끝내고 친구를 만날 거예요 | Once I finish homework, I will meet a friend |
| 일단 피자부터 먹고 과자를 먹을 거예요 | I'll eat the pizza first, then snacks |
