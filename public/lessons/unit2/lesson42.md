## Grammar Points

### Simple Usages of 때

The word **때** means "time." It can be placed after an event or period to refer to the time during that event:

- 저녁 때 = during dinner/evening time
- 방학 때 = during the school vacation
- 회의 때 = during the meeting
- 고등학교 때 = during high school
- 대학교 때 = during university

> Example: 저는 방학 때 집안에서 공부를 할 거예요 = I'm going to study in my house during vacation

### When: … ~ㄹ/을 때

**때** can be used with the **~는 것** principle to indicate a specific moment in which something happens. The future tense form **~ㄹ/을** is usually used to describe 때:

> **내가 밥을 먹을 때** = "the time that I eat" → "When I eat"

- Typically indicates general time or future time
- When the subject of both clauses is the same, you don't write the subject twice

**Examples:**

- 저는 돈을 받을 때 행복할 거예요 = When I receive the money, I will be happy
- 뼈마디가 아플 때 이 약을 드세요 = When your joints hurt, take this medicine
- 머리를 감을 때 비누로 해요 = When I wash my hair, I do it with soap
- 오랫동안 공부할 때 뇌가 아파요 = When I study for a long time, my brain hurts
- 대통령을 만날 때 저는 아주 예의가 바를 거예요 = When I meet the president I will be very polite

> **Important:** The main action occurs precisely at the time indicated by the ~ㄹ/을 때 clause — not after it. When using with 가다 or 오다, the action happens during the going/coming, not after arrival.

### Past Tense: ~았/었을 때

To indicate something happened in the past, add **~았/었** before **~ㄹ/을 때**:

- 먹었을 때 = When I ate
- 갔을 때 = When I went
- 공부했을 때 = When I studied

**Examples:**

- 엄마가 갔을 때 저는 울었어요 = When mom left, I cried
- 제가 어렸을 때 곤충을 먹었어요 = When I was young I used to eat bugs
- 그 연예인의 사인을 받았을 때 너무 설레었어요 = When I got that celebrity's signature, I was really excited

### Particles with 때

The particle **~에** can be added to 때 (and is often omitted). Other particles can attach when 때 functions as subject/object:

- 우리가 지난 번에 만났을 때가 아주 재미있었어요 = The last time we met was really fun
- 저는 사막에서 살았을 때를 잊어버리고 싶어요 = I want to forget the time I lived in the desert
- 키가 클 때까지 얕은 수영장에서만 수영할 거예요 = I'll only swim in shallow pools until I am taller
- 저는 그 팀을 어렸을 때부터 응원했어요 = I've been cheering for that team since I was young
- 내가 한국에 있을 때마다 한국어로 말하고 싶어 = Every time I am in Korea, I want to speak Korean

### "There are times when…" usage

때 can indicate that some "time" or "case" has occurred, often with 있다/없다:

- 그 친구를 죽이고 싶을 때가 있어요 = There are times when I want to kill that friend
- 밤 늦게 배고플 때가 많아요 = There are many times where I am hungry at night
