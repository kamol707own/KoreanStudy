## Key Grammar Points

### Honorific Addition: ~(으)시

Add **~(으)시** to verbs/adjectives when the acting person deserves respect, regardless of who you are speaking to.

- Add **~시** to stems ending in a vowel
- Add **~으시** to stems ending in a consonant

> **Note:** ~(으)시 creates an unconjugated word. You must add a conjugation to it (e.g., ~아/어요, ~ㅂ/습니다, ~았/었다).

**Examples with irregulars:**
| Irregular | Word | + ~(으)시 |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| ㅅ Irregular | 짓다 (to build) | 지으시 |
| ㄷ Irregular | 걷다 (to walk) | 걸으시 |
| ㅂ Irregular | 돕다 (to help) | 도우시 |
| ㄹ Irregular | 살다 (to live) | 사시 |

### Special Words

#### 드리다 and ~께
Use **드리다** instead of 주다 when giving something to someone who deserves high respect. Use **~께** instead of ~에게/한테.

- 할아버지께 선물을 드리고 싶어 = I want to give my grandfather a present

#### 말씀하다
Use **말씀하다** instead of 말하다 when the person speaking deserves high respect.

- 소망이 무엇인지 말씀해 주실래요? = Can you tell me what your dream/hope is?

#### 드시다/잡수시다
Formal versions of "to eat." **잡수시다** shows extreme respect.

- 아버지! 다 드셨어요? = Dad! Have you finished eating?

#### 계시다
Formal equivalent of 있다 when indicating that a person of high respect is in/at a location.

- 아버지가 집에 계셔요 = Dad is (at) home
- **안녕히 계세요** = Goodbye (said to someone who is staying)

#### 주무시다
Formal equivalent of 자다 (to sleep).

- 잘 주무셨어요? = Did you sleep well?

## Example Sentents

- 선생님은 우리를 너무 잘 가르치셨어 = Our teacher taught us really well
- 부장님은 그 집을 나무로만 만드시고 싶었어 = Our boss wanted to make that house using only wood
- 교장선생님이 학교에 안 계셔요 = The principal isn't in the school
- 어떤 할아버지가 저에게 다가오시고 있어요 = Some old man is approaching me
- 저는 항상 손님들께 봉지를 드려요 = I always gives bags to the customers
