## Grammar Points

### 1. A Clause of Uncertainty: ~는지

Add **~는지** to the end of a clause to indicate uncertainty, guessing, or a question embedded in a sentence.

**Structure:** [uncertain clause] + ~는지 + [main verb like 모르다]

> **저는 친구가 어디 가는지 모르겠어요** = I don't know where my friend is going

**When to use:** If the clause contains a question word (누구, 뭐, 언제, 어디, 왜, etc.), ~는지 is automatically used:

- **엄마가 누구랑 먹는지 모르겠어요** = I don't know who mom is eating with
- **엄마가 뭐 먹는지 모르겠어요** = I don't know what mom is eating
- **엄마가 왜 먹는지 모르겠어요** = I don't know why mom is eating

**Without a question word**, ~는지 translates to "if":

- **엄마가 지금 먹고 있는지 모르겠어요** = I don't know if mom is eating now

> **Note:** 모르다 is often replaced by **모르겠어요** ( 모르겠다) for politeness. It has no future tense meaning here.

### 2. Past Tense: ~았/었는지

Add ~았/었 before ~는지 for past uncertain clauses:

- **엄마가 왜 먹었는지 모르겠어요** = I don't know why mom ate
- **열쇠를 어디 두었는지 잊어버렸어요** = I forgot where I put my keys

> **Spoken Korean:** ~ㄴ/은지 is commonly used instead of ~았/었는지 in speech. Both sound natural.

### 3. Future Tense: ~ㄹ/을지

Add ~ㄹ/을 before 지 for future uncertain clauses:

- **택배가 언제 올지 모르겠어요** = I don't know when the delivery will come
- **오후에 비가 올지 모르겠어요** = It might rain in the afternoon

> When no question word is present, "~ㄹ/을지 모르다" often translates to **"might..."**

### 4. ~는지 with Adjectives

For adjective clauses, use **~ㄴ/은지** instead of ~는지:

- **제가 준 것이 괜찮은지 모르겠어요** = I don't know if what I gave is okay
- **이 빛이 충분히 밝은지 모르겠어요** = I don't know if this light is bright enough
- **내일 날씨가 쌀쌀할지 모르겠어요** = I don't know if tomorrow's weather will be chilly

### 5. "If… or not…"

Use two clauses with ~는지 to express "whether or not":

- **내일 영화를 볼지 안 볼지 모르겠어요** = I don't know if I will see a movie tomorrow or not
- **수술을 받을지 안 받을지 확실하지 않아요** = It is not certain if I will get surgery or not

**Different outcomes (not just positive/negative):**

- **내일 공원에 갈지 영화를 볼지 모르겠어요** = I don't know if I'll go to the park or see a movie

> **Tip:** For 하다 verbs, shorten the second clause: 공부했는지 **안 했는지** (not 안 공부했는지)

### 6. Attaching ~도 to ~지

**~ㄹ/을지도 모르다** is commonly used. The ~도 adds nuance of "might or might not," but in practice is essentially the same as ~ㄹ/을지 모르다:

- **내일 비가 올지도 모르겠어요** = It might rain tomorrow
- **내일 비가 올지 모르겠어요** = It might rain tomorrow

> **Usage:** ~도 is only used with future tense uncertain clauses. It is not used with question words.

### 7. Asking "Do you know how to…?"

Use ~지 알아요 to ask if someone knows how to do something:

- **서울에 어떻게 가는지 알아요?** = Do you know how to get to Seoul?
- **그 단어를 어떻게 발음하는지 알아요?** = Do you know how to pronounce that word?

## Example Sentences

- **택배가 언제 올지 모르겠어요** = I don't know when the delivery will come
- **택배로 보내야겠어요** = I'll have to send it by delivery
- **가격을 확인해보자** = Let's check the price
- **용돈을 얼마나 줘야 할지 모르겠어요** = I don't know how much allowance I should give
- **제가 구한 아르바이트가 좋은지 모르겠어요** = I don't know if the job I found is good
- **비상출입구가 어디 있는지 찾았어요** = We found where the emergency exit is
- **정부가 왜 예산을 줄이는지 모르겠어요** = I don't know why the government is cutting the budget
- **그 셔츠를 언제 버렸는지 기억이 안 나요** = I don't remember when I threw away that shirt
- **열쇠를 어디 두었는지 잊어버렸어요** = I forgot where I put my keys
- **이 빛이 충분히 밝은지 모르겠어요** = I don't know if this light is bright enough
- **이 독을 마시면 치료할 수 없어요** = If you drink this poison, you cannot cure it
- **저는 시인이 되려고 노력하고 있어요** = I am trying to become a poet
