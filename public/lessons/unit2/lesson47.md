## Key Grammar

### ~지만 — "Even though" / "But"

Connects two contrasting clauses. Add ~지만 directly to the stem of verbs, adjectives, or 이다. The clause before ~지만 is a fact (already true or certain to happen). No irregulars apply since the addition starts with "ㅈ".

**Present tense:**

> **부산에 가고 싶지만 차가 없어서 못 가요** = Even though I want to go to Busan, I can't go because I don't have a car

> **물을 마셔야 되지만 물이 아직도 다 얼어 있어요** = I need to drink water, but it is still all frozen

**Past tense** (add ~았/었 before ~지만):

> **밥을 먹었지만 배고파요** = Even though I ate, I am hungry

> **상처를 치료했지만 아직 고통이 있어요** = I treated the wound, but there is still pain

**Future tense** (add ~ㄹ/을 것이다 or ~겠다, then attach ~지만 after removing ~다):

> **한국에 안 갈 것이지만 여전히 한국어를 배우고 싶어요** = Even though I will not go to Korea, I still want to learn Korean

> **오늘 선생님이 안 올 거지만 우리는 선생님이 준비하신 내용을 공부해야 돼요** = Even though the teacher won't come today, we need to study the material that she prepared

### 그렇지만 — "However"

Attach ~지만 to 그렇다 to say "Even though (that situation)…" commonly translated as "however."

> **Person 1:** 주차를 이 선 안에 해야 되지 않아요? = Don't you have to park between these lines?
> **Person 2:** 그렇지만 차가 너무 커서 그렇게 할 수 없어요 = However, the car is too big so I can't do it like that.

> **Person 1:** 지금 가야 되지 않아요? = Don't you have to go now?
> **Person 2:** 네, 그렇지만 할 게 많아서 지금 못 가요 = Yes, however I can't go now because I have a lot of things to do

---

> **Key distinction:** With ~지만 the first clause is already an accepted fact. With ~아/어도 (Lesson 48) the first clause is a supposition/uncertain.
