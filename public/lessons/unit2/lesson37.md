## Lesson 37: Because: ~아/어서

### Key Grammar Points

#### V/A + ~아/어서 — Because / Therefore
Connects two clauses to express cause and effect. The reason comes FIRST, then the result.

**Structure:** [Reason] ~아/어서 [Result]

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 저는 배고파**서** 밥을 먹고 싶어요 | Because I am hungry, I want to eat |
| 그 여자가 너무 예뻐**서** 저는 그녀를 만나고 싶어요 | That girl is very pretty, therefore I want to meet her |
| 저는 심심하**서** 공원에 가고 싶어요 | I am bored, therefore I want to go to the park |
| 고속도로가 막혀**서** 일반 길로 갈 거예요 | I will take the normal road because the highway is blocked |

> **Note:** In English, "because" comes in the middle. In Korean, the reason clause comes FIRST. Think of ~아/어서 as "therefore" to match the word order.

---

#### ~아/어서 in the Past Tense
The first clause does NOT take past tense conjugation — tense is inferred from context.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 저는 배고파**서** 밥을 먹었어요 | I was hungry, so I ate |
| 점심을 안 먹**어서** 지금 먹고 있어요 | I didn't eat lunch, so I'm eating now |
| 거기에 안 가 봐**서** 내일 갈 거예요 | I haven't been there, so I will go tomorrow |

---

#### Adding ~아/어서 to 이다
For nouns ending in a consonant: **~이어서** or **~이라(서)**
For nouns ending in a vowel: **~여서** or **~라(서)**

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 일요일이**어서** 공원에 가고 싶어요 | It is Sunday, so I want to go to the park |
| 일요일이**라서** 공원에 가고 싶어요 | (same meaning, more colloquial) |
| 이 방은 원룸이**라서** 너무 작아요 | This is a studio apartment, so it is too small |
| 예쁜 여자**라서** 똑똑하지 않을 것 같아 | She is a pretty girl, so she's probably not smart |

---

#### ~아/어서 in the Future Tense
Same pattern as 이다 since the future tense is ~ㄹ/을 것 + 이다.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 나중에 밥이 없을 **거라서** 지금 먹고 싶어요 | There will be no food later, so I want to eat now |
| 친구가 거기에 많을 **거라서** 그 파티에 가고 싶어요 | Many friends will be there, so I want to go to that party |

---

#### 그래서 = Therefore / That's Why
~아/어서 added to **그렇다** (like that). Used to connect a previously mentioned situation to a result.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 비가 왔어요? — 네, **그래서** 나가기 싫어요 | Did it rain? — Yeah, that's why I don't want to go out |
| 한국의 주식이 쌀이에요. **그래서** 밀가루보다 쌀로 만든 음식이 더 많아요 | Korea's staple is rice. Therefore, there are more rice-based foods |

---

#### 왜냐하면 = Because (conjunction word)
Less common than ~아/어서. More formal/written. Often paired with **때문에**.

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 저는 한국을 사랑해요, **왜냐하면** 한국 사람들이 예의가 바르**기 때문이에요** | I love Korea because Korean people are very polite |
