## Key Grammar

### ~아/어도 — "Regardless of…"

Attach ~아/어도 to the stem of a verb or adjective. The first clause is a **supposition** (uncertain), and the second clause occurs regardless of whether the first happens. This is the key difference from ~지만 (where the first clause is already a fact).

**With verbs:**

> **태풍이 와도 내일 박람회에 가야 돼요** = Regardless of whether there is a typhoon, I have to go to the trade show tomorrow

> **보석을 그 동굴에 숨겨도 사람들이 찾을 거예요** = Regardless of whether you hide the jewels in that cave, people will find them

**With adjectives:**

> **여자들이 예뻐도 똑똑하지 않으면 매력이 없어요** = Regardless of how pretty girls are, if they are not smart, they have no charm

> **강가가 멀어도 우리는 거기 내일 가야 돼요** = Regardless of if the river is far, we have to go there tomorrow

**With 이다:**

> **남자이어도** / **남자이라도** / **남자여도** / **남자라도** — all acceptable forms

> **그 남자가 마법사라도 그 문을 열 수 없을 거예요** = Regardless of if that man is a magician, he won't be able to open that door

### Question words with ~아/어도

Since the first clause is a supposition, question words can be used:

> **네가 어디 가도 나는 가고 싶지 않아** = Regardless of where you are going, I don't want to go

> **이 제품을 언제 구입해도 그 가격을 내야 돼요** = Regardless of when you purchase this product, you have to pay that price

### 아무리 — "No matter how much"

Replaces 얼마나 in sentences with ~아/어도 to express "no matter how much":

> **네가 나를 아무리 사랑해도 우리는 헤어져야 돼** = Regardless of how much you love me, we need to break up

> **아무리 그 제품을 반값으로 줘도 저는 안 살 거예요** = Regardless of if you give me that product at half price, I'm not going to buy it

### 그래도 — "Regardless" / "It doesn't matter"

Attach ~아/어도 to 그렇다 to create 그래도:

> **Person 1:** 같이 가자! 사람이 많을 거야! = Let's go together! There will be so many people
> **Person 2:** 그래도 가기 싫어요 = Regardless. I'm not going.

> **Person 1:** 선생님! 배가 좀 아파요! = Teacher! My stomach is a little sore
> **Person 2:** 그래도 수업에 와야 돼 = Regardless, you have to come to class

---

> **Key distinction:** ~지만 marks an already-known fact; ~아/어도 marks a hypothetical/supposition. Question words go with ~아/어도, not ~지만.
