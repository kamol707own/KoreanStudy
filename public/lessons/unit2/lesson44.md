## Grammar Points

### Let's…: ~자 (informal)

Add **~자** to the stem of a verb to suggest doing something together ("Let's…"). Always present tense; no irregular changes. Informal — only for friends or people younger than you.

**Examples:**

- 밥을 먹자! = Let's eat!
- 내일 공원에 가자! = Tomorrow, let's go to the park!
- 무대에 같이 올라가자 = Let's go onto the stage together
- 쪽지를 서로 보내자 = Let's send messages to each other

**Let's not:** Attach ~자 to ~지 말다:

- 내일 공원에 가지 말자 = Let's not go to the park tomorrow

### Let's…: ~ㅂ/읍시다 (slightly formal)

More formal version of ~자. **~읍시다** for consonant stems, **~ㅂ시다** for vowel stems:

- 밥을 먹읍시다! = Let's eat!
- 내일 공원에 갑시다! = Tomorrow, let's go to the park!
- 그 규정을 내일부터 시행합시다 = Let's enforce that rule starting tomorrow
- 연수를 받으러 갑시다 = Let's go to receive the training

> Appropriate for offices or when some respect is owed. Avoid with someone deserving very high respect — use a question instead (같이 먹고 싶습니까?).

### ~ㄹ/을래(요)

This ending has four distinct usages:

#### Usage 1: Shall we…? (asking if listener wants to do something together)

- 내일 공부하러 독서실에 갈래요? = Shall we go to the library tomorrow to study?
- 영화를 볼래요? = Shall we see a movie together?
- 영어숙어를 같이 외워 볼래? = Shall we try to memorize English idioms together?

With question words:

- 언제 할래? = When shall we do it?
- 어디 갈래? = Where shall we go?
- 뭐 먹을래? = What shall we eat?

#### Usage 2: Do you want me to…? (empty threats)

- 맞을래? = Do you want to be hit?
- 죽을래? = Do you want to die?

#### Usage 3: Could you please…? (requesting a favor)

Attach to 주다 (often with ~아/어 주다 or honorific ~아/어 주시다):

- 김치를 더 주실래요? = Could you please give me more kimchi?
- 문을 닫아 주실래요? = Could you please close the door?
- 환불해 주실래요? = Could you please give me a refund?
- 에어컨을 틀어 주실래요? = Could you please turn on the air conditioner?

> Softer than a direct command — feels more like a polite request.

#### Usage 4: I want to / I'm going to (speaker's intention)

Similar to ~고 싶다 or simple future tense, but only used for simple sentences:

- 나는 집에 갈래 = I'm going home / I want to go home
- 나는 그거를 먹을래 = I'm going to eat that / I want to eat that
- 나는 안 할래 = I'm not going to do it / I don't want to do it
