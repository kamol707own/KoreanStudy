# (Theme) Unit 1: #1 – School

## Introduction

The majority of foreign learners of Korean are teachers or exchange students in Korea. Even if you don't fit into those categories, you are likely a student somewhere. The word "class" as it relates to schools is difficult for English speakers — each meaning has a different Korean word:

- **반** = this refers to the group of people (e.g., "There are 30 students in my class")
- **수업** = this refers to the lesson that you receive (e.g., "We had 5 classes today")
- **교실** = this refers to the classroom (e.g., "He is in the classroom")
- **교시** = this refers to the period of time (usually translated as "period")

> Example: Our **반** had PE **수업** in the **교실** during second **교시**

## Sentences

### Being a teacher

- 저는 선생님이에요 = I am a teacher
- 저는 영어회화 선생님이에요 = I am an English conversation teacher
- 저는 영어 선생님이에요 = I am an English teacher
- 저는 곧 선생님이 될 것입니다 = I will become a teacher soon
- 저는 선생님이 되고 싶어요 = I want to be/become a teacher
- 저는 언젠가 선생님이 되고 싶어요 = I want to become a teacher someday
- 담임선생님은 누구예요? = Who is your homeroom teacher?
- 저는 한국에서 영어 회화선생님이에요 = I am an English conversation teacher in Korea
- 무슨 과목을 가르쳐요? = What subject do you teach?
- 선생님이 왜 되고 싶었어요? = Why did you want to become a teacher?
- 이 학교에서 언제부터 일했어요? = Since when have you been at this school?
- 이 학교 일을 어떻게 구했어요? = How did you find this position?
- 학교에서 몇 시에 퇴근해요? = What time do you leave school?
- 집에 몇 시에 가요? = What time will you go home?
- 1주일에 수업을 몇 개 가르쳐요? = How many classes do you teach per week?
- 하루에 (보통) 수업을 몇 개 가르쳐요? = How many classes do you (usually) teach per day?
- 이 사람은 우리 학교(의) 영어 원어민 선생님이에요 = This person is our school's native English teacher
- 교장선생님은 선생님들에게 존경 받아요 = The principal is respected by the teachers
- 학생들은 수업 시간 동안 자고 있었어요 = The students were sleeping during class
- 그 사람이 선생님인지 아닌지 몰라요 = I don't know if that person is a teacher or not

- 만약 제가 선생님이라면 학생들에게 존경을 받고 싶을 거에요 = If I were a teacher, I would want to be respected by students
- 이렇게 많은 내용을 학생들에게 가르치려고 고생을 많이 했어요 = I worked hard in order to teach that much content to the students

- 열심히 공부해요! = Study hard!

- 시험을 잘 봤어요? = Did you do well on the exam?

- 시험을 망했어요! = I messed up the exam!

## Korean Schools

The school year starts at the beginning of March, divided into two semesters. The first semester usually ends in July, followed by about a one-month summer vacation. School starts again in August and runs until the end of December, before a two-month winter vacation.

Korean students attend elementary school from ages 8 to 13 (grades 1–6). After elementary school, students move to middle school (grades 1–3), then three years of high school (grades 1–3). Competition to get into the best schools is incredibly fierce. Korean students study constantly to improve their grades.

The school day usually runs from around 8:00am to around 4:00pm, and most students continue studying after school by attending a **학원** (private academy). During the day, students sit through lessons with very little activity or hands-on learning. Most schools focus on exams, and teaching not related to solving exam questions is often seen as a waste of time.

A typical semester might have one or two **수행평가** (performance assessments), which normally constitute about 30% of a final grade, with the rest from midterm and final exams.

Students are ranked with their peers rather than measured by absolute scores. The three years of high school end when students write the **수능** (Korean SAT) in mid-November. Most university admission is based on this one test per year, putting students under extreme pressure.
