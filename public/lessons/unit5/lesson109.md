## To guess, think, suppose, to look like: ~ㄴ가/은가/는가/나 보다

By attaching ~ㄴ가/은가/는가/나 보다 to words at the end of a sentence, the speaker indicates that the sentence is a **guess**, and is thought to be **true based on evidence**.

### How it attaches:

- **~ㄴ가 보다** is attached to **이다** and **adjectives where the stem ends in a vowel**
  - 여기가 휴게실**인가 봐요** = I guess this is the resting room
  - 시험이 어려운가 봐요 = I guess the exam is hard

- **~은가 보다** is attached to **adjectives where the stem ends in a consonant**
  - 그 사람이 싫**은가 봐요** = I guess he doesn't like that person

- **~는가 보다** is attached to **all verbs** and all versions of **있다/없다**
  - 온수가 잘 안 나오**는가 봐요** = I guess hot water doesn't really work well
  - 애기가 지금 욕조에 **있는가 봐요** = I guess the baby is in the bathtub

- **~나 보다** can be attached to **anything** (이다, adjectives, or verbs)
  - 여기가 휴게실**이나 봐요** = I guess this is the resting room
  - 시험이 어렵**나 봐요** = I guess the exam is hard
  - 온수가 잘 안 나오**나 봐요** = I guess hot water doesn't really work well

> In reality, I would almost always use ~ㄴ/은가 보다 with adjectives and 이다, and ~나 보다 with verbs.

### Key Difference from ~ㄹ/을 것 같다:

~ㄹ/을 것 같다 = guess based on **intuition** (no immediate evidence)
~ㄴ가/은가/는가/나 보다 = guess based on **direct evidence**

> **시험이 어려울 것 같아요** = The exam will probably be hard (intuition)
> **시험이 어려운가 봐요** = I guess the exam is hard (you see students sighing during the test)

### With past tense:

~나 보다 is attached to ~었~, regardless of verb, adjective or 이다:

- 미로가 어렵진 않았**나 봐요** = I guess the maze wasn't difficult
- 빨래를 아직 안 헹겼**나 봐요** = I guess the laundry hasn't rinsed yet
- 누가 그 제품의 단점을 지적하지 않았**나 봐요** = I guess nobody pointed out that product's flaws
- 경찰관이 엎드린 사람을 보고 죽은 것으로 추정했**나 봐요** = It seems as though the police presumed he was dead
- 건물에 아무도 없는 것을 보니 모든 사람들이 지진에 잘 대피했**나 봐요** = I guess they all were able to evacuate from the earthquake
- 곰이 걷는 것이 어색해 하는 것을 보니 평생 우리에 갇혀 있었**나 봐요** = I guess he was trapped in a cage his whole life
- 쓰레기가 이렇게 무더기로 쌓여 있는데 한번도 청소를 안 했**나 봐요** = I guess they never cleaned the place even once
- 그 사람이 오랫동안 헌신해서 어머니를 돌봐서 어머니가 거의 다 나았**나 봐** = It looks like she is almost better
- 급하게 가다가 접시를 바닥에 엎어서 다시 요리해야 되겠**나 봐요** = It looks like I'll have to cook again

> "How was the date?"
> "음~ 괜찮았는데 남자가 조금 못생겼어 ㅠ" = Oh… it was okay, but the guy was a little bit ugly
> "아 진짜? 그 건 안 돼~" = Really? That's not good!

### Adding ~네(요):

~네(요) is often added to sentences where the speaker learns something new. It is very common to add ~네(요) to 보다:

- 시험이 어려운가 보**네요** = I guess the exam is hard
- 사과가 너무 비싸나 보**네요** = I guess the apples are too expensive
- 내복을 입어야 되는가 보**네** = I guess I need to wear long-johns
- 아기가 접시를 깨뜨렸나 보**네** = I guess the baby broke the plate
- 수신이 안 됐나 보**네** = I guess there is no reception
