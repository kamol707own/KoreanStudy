## Recalling a Fact from Experience: ~더~

The grammatical principle **~더~** signifies that the speaker is recalling (or requesting that somebody recall) some fact from the past that was **experienced**. You will learn three grammatical principles that include ~더~:

- ~더~ + ~ㄴ/가 = **~던가** (for example: 아프던가)
- ~더~ + ~라 = **~더라** (for example: 아프더라)
- ~더~ + (으)니 = **~더니** (for example: 아프더니)

---

## Asking Questions to Yourself Using ~던가

~던가 is used when the speaker is asking a **yes/no question to him or herself** about something that was experienced. It is usually used when the speaker is unsure of something that was experienced, and therefore something that probably should be known.

- 내가 이 책을 **읽었던가**? = Did/have I read this book?

### More examples:

- 파리를 **죽였던가**? = Did I kill the fly?
- 이 옷을 **빨았던가**? = Did I wash these clothes?
- 그 강좌를 **들었던가**? = Did I take that class?
- 어제 설거지를 **했던가**? = Did I do the dishes yesterday?
- 숫자 "3"이 홀수**이던가**? = Is "3" an odd number?
- 옥상에 올라가도 **되던가**? = Am I allowed to go to the rooftop?
- 이자가 원래 이렇게 높**던가**? = Was the interest always this high?
- 서울 사람들이 표준어를 **쓰던가**? = Do people in Seoul use the standard language?
- 아빠가 이 선물을 나에게 **줬던가**? = Did dad give me this present?

### With question words (ambiguous meaning only):

- 내가 이 책을 **언제** 읽었던가? = Did I read this book at some time?
- 그 사람이 **어디** 갔던가? = Did that person go to some place?
- 오빠가 나한테 **뭐**라고 했던가? = Did my brother say something to me?

---

## Asking Questions to Somebody Else Using ~던가

This form can be used informally to ask a question to another person (usually older asking younger), but it is not commonly used:

- 슬기가 많이 **아프던가**? = Is Seulgi really sick?
- 네가 생선을 **먹던가**? = Do you eat fish?
- 네가 핸드폰을 **샀던가**? = Did you buy a cell-phone?
