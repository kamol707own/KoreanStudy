## I said that…: ~ㄴ/는다니까

**~ㄴ/는다니까** expresses **frustration or anger** at the fact that the speaker needs to repeat what was already said. It is rude – use only with people of equal or lower status.

This is a contraction of **~ㄴ/는다고 하니까** (quoted sentence + 하다 + ~(으)니까).

### Present tense:

- **한다니까** = I'm not doing it! (contraction of 한다고 하니까)
- **간다니까** = I'm going! (contraction of 간다고 하니까)
- **먹는다니까** = I'm eating! (contraction of 먹는다고 하니까)

### Examples:

- 출근해야 **된다니까**! = I have to go to work!
- 아직 안 **익었다니까**! = They haven't ripened yet!
- 거스름돈을 안 **줬다니까**! = You didn't give me my change!
- 책이 내용 대로 **분류되었다니까** = I already told you that the books are divided by content
- 지금 **명상한다니까**! 들어오지 마! = I told you not to come in! I'm meditating!
- 그렇게 하면 벌금이 계속 **누적된다니까**! = If you do it like that, the fee will keep accumulating!
- 반이상 정답을 맞추면 합격으로 **간주한다니까** = I already said that if you get more than half correct then it is considered a pass
- 경기가 동점으로 **끝났다니까**! = I already said that the game ended in a tie!

### Past tense:

- **했다니까** = I did it! (contraction of 했다고 하니까)
- **갔다니까** = I went! (contraction of 갔다고 하니까)
- **먹었다니까** = I ate! (contraction of 먹었다고 하니까)

### Future tense:

- **하겠다니까** = I'll do it! (contraction of 하겠다고 하니까)
- **가겠다니까** = I'll go! (contraction of 가겠다고 하니까)

> Person 1: 돈을 언제 줄 거야? = When are you going to give me the money?
> Person 2: 내일 **주겠다니까**! = I will give it to you tomorrow!

## With Adjectives

The present tense plain/diary form of adjectives is identical to the dictionary form. Therefore, ~니까 is placed directly after the dictionary form:

> Person 1: 영어를 잠깐 해 봐 = Try speaking in English
> Person 2: **싫다니까**! = I don't want to!

> Person 1: 이 걸 버릴게 = I'm going to throw this out!
> Person 2: 그 걸 먹고 싶다**니까**! = I wanted to eat that!

> Person 1: 내일 가고 싶은 사람이 있어? = Is there anybody who wants to go tomorrow?
> Person 2: **있다니까** = I already said there is somebody who wants to go!

### More examples:

- 이런 빵은 너무 두껍다**니까** = I told you that this kind of bread is too thick
- 그 사람은 보면 볼수록 예쁘다**니까** = I told you that the more you look at that person, the more pretty she is

---

## With 이다

이다 should be changed to (이)라, just like when quoting:

- **이라니까** = contraction of (이)라고 하니까

> Person 2: **부채라니까**! = It is a "부채"!

### Examples:

- 이 운동은 건강한 몸매를 위한 운동**이라니까** 모두가 해 보는 것을 추천해 = I said that this exercise is good for a good figure, so I recommend everybody try it
- 이게 그냥 속담**이라니까** 그러니 이 문장의 문법을 그렇게까지 공부 안 해도 돼 = I told you it's just a proverb, so you don't need to study the grammar to that extent

## With other Grammatical Principles

### With ~자 (let's…):

- **하자니까** = contraction of ~자고 하니까

> Person 1: 빨리 가자! = Let's go quickly!
> Person 2: 잠깐만… 이것만 하고…
> Person 1: 빨리 **가자니까**! = Let's go quickly! (I already said)

### With ~(으)라 (commands):

- **하라니까** = contraction of ~(으)라고 하니까

> Person 1: 밥을 천천히 먹어! = Eat slowly!
> Person 2: *(eats quickly)*
> Person 1: 천천히 **먹으라니까** = Eat slowly!

### Examples:

- **사실 여부를 확인하라니까**! = I told you to check the RSVP!
- 회사에서는 이렇게 단순한 일을 하지 **말라니까** = I told you to stop doing such simple tasks at work
- 그렇게 하면 화재가 날 거**라니까**! 이제 그만해! = I told you that if you do that you'll start a fire! Stop now!
- 한 사람에게 다 주지 말고 균등하게 **배분하라니까** = I told you to not give it all to one person, but to divide them evenly
- 자꾸 잘난 척 하지 **말라니까** 너무 거만한 거 아니야? = I told you to stop pretending you did well, aren't you being too arrogant
