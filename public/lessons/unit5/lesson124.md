## To be Worth Doing: ~ㄹ/을 만하다

**~ㄹ/을 만하다** indicates that something is **worth doing** or **possible**. The preceding clause describes **만하다** (which is an adjective).

### Basic structure:

- 할 **만하다** = to be worth doing

### Examples:

- 그것은 할 **만해요** = That is worth doing
- 한국은 살 **만해요**? = Is it worth living in Korea?
- 그 밥은 먹을 **만해요** = That food is worth eating
- 그 책은 읽을 **만해요** = That book is worth reading
- 그 컴퓨터는 살 **만해요** = That computer is worth buying
- 그 사람은 믿을 **만해요** = That person is worth trusting
- 코코아는 맛볼 **만해요** = Cocoa is worth tasting

### With ~아/어 보다 (attempting):

- 그 밥은 먹어 볼 **만해요** = That food is worth trying
- 그 책은 읽어 볼 **만해요** = That book is worth reading (worth attempting)

### Common phrase:

> 밥은 먹을 **만해**? = Is the food worth eating? / Is the food good?

### With 받다 (deserves/is worthy of):

- 그 선생님은 상을 받을 **만해요** = That teacher deserves an award
- 그 의사는 많은 사람의 목숨을 살려서 칭찬을 많이 받을 **만해요** = That doctor deserves a lot of praise
- 우리 할아버지는 한국전쟁에서 싸우셨으므로 존경을 받을 **만해요** = Our grandfather deserves respect

### More examples:

- 집중력을 기르기 위해 사격을 배울 **만 한가요**? = Is it worth learning to shoot to increase concentration?
- 다리에 쥐가 자꾸 나는데 병원에 갈 **만할까요**? = I keep getting a cramp, is it worth going to the hospital?
- 그 원룸 방세도 비싸고 7평 밖에 안 되는데 그래도 살 **만해요**? = The rent is high and it's only 7 pyeong, do you think it's worth living in?

---

## ~ㄹ/을 만한 + noun

As an adjective, ~ㄹ/을 만하다 can describe an upcoming noun:

- **먹을 만한** 식당을 알아요? = Do you know a worthwhile place to eat?
- **읽을 만한** 데를 알아요? = Do you know a good place to read a book?
- 살 **만한** 청바지가 있어요 = There are jeans worth buying
- 수다를 떨 **만한** 장소가 많아요 = There are many places worth chatting at
- 그 시골에 야영할 **만한** 데가 있어요? = Are there any places worth camping in that town?
- 보름 동안 유럽에 여행할 **만한** 나라가 있어요? = Are there any places worth traveling in Europe for a fortnight?
- 그 피난민들이 가족과 이별해서 갈 **만한** 데가 없어요 = Those refugees have nowhere to go

---

## Using 가치 to Express a Similar Meaning

The noun **가치** (value/worth) can express the same meaning:

- 그 영화를 세 시간 동안 볼 **가치가** 있었어요 = It was worth watching that movie for three hours
- 이 커피가 5천원을 내고 마실 **가치가** 있을까요? = Is this coffee worth 5,000 won?

### Combined with ~ㄹ/을 만하다:

- 외국어를 배울 만한 **가치가** 있어요 = It is worth learning a foreign language
- 해외에서 한 번이라도 살아볼 만한 **가치가** 있어요 = It is worth trying living in a foreign country
- 이 묘지 자리가 이렇게 많은 돈을 낼 만한 **가치가** 있어요? = Is it worth paying this much for this cemetery plot?
