## To decide to do: ~기로 하다

By attaching ~기로 하다 to the end of a clause, the speaker indicates that he/she has "decided" to do that action.

The verb before ~기로 is always conjugated in the **present tense**, even if the action was decided in the past or will happen in the future.

### Examples

- **저는 선생님이 되기로 했어요** = I decided to become a teacher
- **내일 친구랑 음악회에 같이 가기로 했어요** = I decided to go to the concert with my friend tomorrow
- **애기가 이제 하품을 많이 해서 집에 곧 가기로 했어요** = I decided to go home soon because the baby is yawning a lot
- **이제 그 일을 다 해내서 한 달 동안 쉬기로 했어요** = Now that I have finished all of that work, I decided to rest for a month

### Deciding not to do

- **저는 그 여자를 아주 좋아하지만 짝사랑이라서 얘기를 안 하기로 했어요** = I really like that girl, but it is a one-sided love, so I decided to not talk with her

### Supposed to do (when plan changes)

- **원래 떡볶이를 먹기로 했는데 친구 한 명이 매운 것을 못 먹어서 다른 것을 먹었어요** = We were supposed to eat 떡볶이, but we ate something different
