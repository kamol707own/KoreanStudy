## To do something after another: ~다가 (present tense)

When the verb before ~다가 is in the present tense, the sentence implies that this action was **halted (or cut short)**.

### Examples

- **학생은 열심히 공부하다가 잠이 들었어요** = The student studied hard and then fell asleep
- **요리를 하다가 주방에서 불이 나서 급하게 가스레인지를 껐어요** = I was cooking, and then a fire started so I turned off the stove
- **침실에서 자다가 갑자기 돌아가셨어요** = While sleeping in the bedroom, he suddenly passed away

---

## Completed action: ~았/었다가

When ~았/었 is added, the action before ~다가 completed before the next action started.

- **해물을 먹었다가 토했어요** = I ate seafood and then threw up
- **임산부가 전철을 탔다가 자리가 없어서 전철에서 내렸어요** = The pregnant woman got on the subway, and because there was no seat, she got off
- **날씨가 영하 10도까지 떨어졌다가 다시 영상으로 올랐어요** = The weather fell to 10 degrees below zero, and then went back up above zero

### With 가다 and 오다

- **학교에 가다가 친구 집에 갔어요** = While going to school, I went to a friend's house (did NOT finish going to school)
- **학교에 갔다가 친구 집에 갔어요** = I went to school, then went to a friend's house (DID finish going to school)

---

## Adding ~다가 to ~에

~에다가 can be used instead of ~에 with identical meaning.

- **밥을 냉장고에넣어** = Put the rice in the fridge
- **밥을 냉장고에다가 넣어** = Put the rice in the fridge

~에다가 is more likely used when one thing is being **added** to something else.
