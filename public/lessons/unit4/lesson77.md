## The Meaning of 그런데

**그런데** is created from 그렇다 + ~ㄴ데. The most common English translation is "by the way," but this isn't perfect.

그런데 is used when the speaker acknowledges the situation (through 그렇다) and the use of ~는데 sets up the next clause by indicating it will go in another direction.

### Examples

- **집을 다 청소했어요 / 그런데 바닥을 언제 닦았어요?** = I cleaned the whole house / But... when did you wipe the floors?

**근데** is a contraction of 그런데 and is more common in speech.

---

## Finishing a Sentence with ~는데

~는데 can end a sentence where the remainder can be assumed from context. This creates a similar meaning to "But..."

### Examples

- **가기 싫은데…** = But I don't want to go…
- **맛없는데…** = But it's not delicious…
- **너무 큰데** = But it's too big
- **빗자루가 없는데요** = But there is no broom

This usage is commonly used when disagreeing with what somebody says.

## ~는 데: To Take an Amount of Time or Resources

When describing "데" using ~는, the speaker can indicate that it takes a certain amount of time (or resources) to complete an action.

### Examples

- **밥을 다 먹는 데 한 시간 걸렸어요** = It took an hour to eat all the food
- **여기까지 오는 데 시간이 많이 걸렸어요** = It took a long time for me to come here
- **제가 우리 집에서 친구의 집까지 가는 데 5분 걸렸어요** = It took 5 minutes for me to get from my house to my friend's house
- **집을 짓는 데 돈이 많이 들었어요** = It cost a lot to have this house built
