## As soon as: ~자마자

By attaching ~자마자 to a verb at the end of a clause, the speaker indicates that an action occurs "as soon as" another action does.

### Examples

- **버스가 출발하자마자 사람들은 움직였어요** = As soon as the bus moved, people started moving
- **경찰관들은 범죄자를 찾자마자 기소할 거예요** = As soon as the police find the criminal, they will prosecute him/her
- **비행기 바퀴가 땅에 닿자마자 승객들이 자리에서 일어났어요** = As soon as the plane's wheels hit the ground, the passengers got out of their seats

---

## As soon as: ~는 대로

대로 can also have a similar meaning as ~자마자.

- **집에 도착하는 대로 밥을 먹어야 돼요** = As soon as we arrive at home, I need to eat

~자마자 is much more common than ~는 대로 in conversation.

---

## Three meanings of ~자

### 1. As soon as (when actions have already happened)

- **버스가 출발하자 사람들은 움직였어요** = As soon as the bus moved, people started moving

### 2. Cause-and-effect

- **바람이 불자 그 집이 쓰러질 것 같아요** = The house might fall down because of the wind blowing

### 3. Two things at once (with 이다)

- **저는 선생님이자 학생이에요** = I am a teacher and a student
- **이것은 처음이자 마지막 기회예요** = This is the first and last chance
