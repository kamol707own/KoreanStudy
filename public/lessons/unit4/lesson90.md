## The meaning of ~잖아(요)

~잖아(요) creates a meaning that loosely translates to "as you know" or "don't forget that..."

**The listener should already know (or should already know) the fact that you are stating.**

### Examples

- **맞잖아요!** = I told you it was correct! You should have known!
- **내일은 토요일이잖아요!** = You should know that tomorrow is Saturday!
- **나는 돈이 없잖아!** = Don't forget that I don't have any money!
- **윌리가 배고프잖아!** = Don't forget that Willy eats a lot!

### When describing a story

Provide background information that is common knowledge:

- **제가 어제 회사에서 조금 아팠잖아요…… 그래서 집에 가서 밥을 먹고 갑자기 토했어요** = As you know I was sick at work yesterday... Then I went home and threw up

### Warning: Can sound rude

~잖아(요) can sound like you are saying "I TOLD you it was like that!" Be cautious with elders.

For more polite alternatives, consider using ~알다시피:

- **부장님도 아시다시피 저는 어제 아파서 회사에 늦게 왔어요** = As you know, I was sick yesterday, so I was late

### 있잖아 / 있잖아요

Used at the beginning of sentences when thinking of what to say:

- **있잖아… 오늘은 재활용을 해야 되는 날이야** = So, you know... Today is the day to do recycling
