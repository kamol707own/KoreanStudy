## To know (how to): ~ㄹ/을 줄 알다

- **각도를 잴 줄 알아요** = I know how to measure the angle
- **김밥을 만들 줄 알아요** = I know how to make 김밥

With 모르다:

- **각도를 잴 줄 몰라요** = I don't know how to measure the angle

### Stating one realized something is not true

When describing 줄, the speaker states that something is not true but originally thought it was true.

- **미안해요~ 학생인 줄 알았어요** = Sorry, I thought you were a student
- **아빠가 풀을 이미 벤 줄 알았어요** = I thought dad already cut the grass
- **내가 죽을 줄 알았어** = I thought I was going to die

With 모르다:

- **거기가 비싼 줄 몰랐어요** = I didn't know it was expensive there

## As you know: ~다시피

~다시피 is commonly added to 알다 to refer to what the listener knows.

- **너도 알다시피…** = As you know…
- **선생님도 아시다시피…** = As you (teacher) know…

With 보다:

- **너도 보다시피…** = As you (can) see…
- **선생님도 보시다시피…** = As you (teacher) (can) see…

### Examples

- **부장님도 아시다시피 저는 내일 출장을 가야 돼요** = As you know, I have to go on a business trip tomorrow
- **너도 보다시피 우리 집 상황이 매우 안 좋아** = As you can see, the situation of our house is really not good
