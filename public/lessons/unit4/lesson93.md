## Turn a Statement into a Question using ~지/~죠

~지 (informal) and ~죠 (formal) turn a statement into a question, similar to "eh?" in Canadian English.

### Examples

- **날씨가 추워요** = It is cold
- **날씨가 춥죠?** = It is cold, isn't it?
- **날씨가 춥지?** = It is cold, isn't it?
- **선생님! 바쁘죠?** = Teacher! You are busy, aren't you?
- **가기 싫지?** = You don't want to go, don't you?

### Past Tense

- **어제 학교에 안 갔죠?** = You didn't go to school yesterday, did you?

### Future Tense

- **선생님도 갈 거죠?** = Teacher, you are going too, right?

### With Question Words

- **카드를 어디에 두었죠?** = Where did you put the card?
- **점심으로 뭐 먹었죠?** = What did you eat for lunch?

The speaker sort of knows the answer and is reconfirming.

---

## Of Course!

~지/~죠 can respond to questions meaning "of course":

- **내일 갈 거야!? / 갈 거지** = Are you going tomorrow? / Yeah, of course I'm going
- **배고파? / 배고프지!** = Are you hungry? / Of course I'm hungry!
- **당연하지!!** = Of course!!

---

## Using ~지/~죠 with ~아/어야 하다/되다

~지 can replace 하다/되다 to mean "must":

- **나는 지금 가야지!** = I must go now
- **열심히 공부를 해야지!** = You need to study hard!
