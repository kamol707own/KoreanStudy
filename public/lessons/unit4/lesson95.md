## Attaching 뿐 to a noun

뿐 indicates there is nothing other than the noun it is attached to.

### Examples

- **내가 원하는 유일한 것은 너뿐이야** = The only thing I want is you
- **그 창고에 있는 것은 짝퉁뿐이에요** = The only thing in that warehouse is fakes
- **사람들이 나무뿌리를 다 파내서 여기 있는 게 풀뿐이야** = People dug up all roots, so the only thing left here is grass

### ~뿐만 아니라 (Not only A, but also B)

- **커피뿐만 아니라 간식도 샀어요** = Not only coffee, but also snacks
- **곰팡이뿐만 아니라 벌레도 있어요** = Not only mold, but bugs too

---

## Describing 뿐 with a verb/adjective/이다

When described by something, 뿐 means "just/only" with a nuance that there's nothing special about it.

### Examples

- **그냥 돈일 뿐이에요** = It's just money
- **그것은 농담일 뿐이에요** = It's just a joke
- **청소를 그렇게 하면 겉에만 깨끗해질 뿐이야** = If you clean like that, only the outside will be clean

### In the middle of a sentence

- **그녀는 저를 쳐다봤을 뿐 그냥 앉아 있었어요** = She just sat there staring at me
- **그 남자는 손톱을 물어뜯을 뿐 전화를 받지 않았어요** = He just bit his fingernails and didn't answer the phone

---

## Not only..., but also: ~ㄹ/을 뿐만 아니라

- **그는 가난할 뿐만 아니라 못생겼어요** = He is not only poor, but ugly
- **이 회사는 집을 제공할 뿐만 아니라 차도 제공해요** = Not only housing, but also a car
