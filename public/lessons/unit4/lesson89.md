## To compare: 비교하다

비교하다 is an actual verb meaning "to compare," different from the particle ~보다.

- **저를 그 사람과 비교하지 마세요** = Don't compare me with that person

---

## Comparing with 비하다

Attach ~에 to a noun and place 비해(서) after it:

- **우리 아들은 또래에 비해 훨씬 똑똑해요** = Our son is much smarter compared to his peers
- **한국어는 영어에 비해 모음이 더 많아요** = There are more vowels in Korean compared to English

---

## Orders of Magnitude: 배

배 acts as a counter for orders of magnitude. Use pure Korean numbers.

- **두 배** = twice
- **세 배** = three times
- **네 배** = four times

### Examples

- **저는 저의 남동생보다 두 배 더 강해요** = I am twice as strong as my younger brother
- **이 자판기가 편의점보다 식품을 세 배 더 비싸게 팔아요** = This vending machine sells food for three times the price

---

## Korean Fractions

The denominator is said first, the numerator last, with 분의 between:

- **2분의 1** = one half
- **4분의 1** = one quarter
- **5분의 2** = two fifths

### Examples

- **캐나다가 특이한 게 인구의 1/4은 이민자예요** = The unique thing about Canada is that the population is one-quarter immigrants
- **우유의 성분의 1/5은 물이에요** = One fifth of milk's components is water
