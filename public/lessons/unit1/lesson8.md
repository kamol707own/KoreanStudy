## Korean Adverbs

Adverbs tell you **when**, **where**, or **to what degree** something is being done.

### "When and Where" Adverbs

When placing a word in a sentence that indicates when or where, you must add the particle **에**.

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Places get ~에 | 병원**에** 갔어요 (I went to the hospital) |
| Times get ~에 | 화요일**에** 가겠어요 (I will go on Tuesday) |
| 오늘, 내일, 어제 do NOT get ~에 | 오늘 도착했어요 (I arrived today) |

> Adverbs can be placed almost anywhere in a sentence except at the very end (which must be a verb or adjective).

### "To What Degree" Adverbs

These adverbs are typically placed **immediately before the verb**:

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 저는 친구를 **자주** 만나요 | I meet my friend **often** |
| 저는 밥을 **많이** 먹었어요 | I ate **a lot** of rice |
| 저는 집에 **바로** 갔어요 | I went home **immediately** |
| 저는 숙제를 **빨리** 했어요 | I did my homework **quickly** |

### Making Adverbs from Adjectives

| Rule | Adjective | Adverb |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Add ~게 | 쉽다 (easy) | 쉽게 (easily) |
| Add ~게 | 비슷하다 (similar) | 비슷하게 (similarly) |
| Add ~게 | 다르다 (different) | 다르게 (differently) |
| Change 하다 to ~히 | 조용하다 (quiet) | 조용히/조용하게 (quietly) |
| Change 하다 to ~히 | 안전하다 (safe) | 안전히/안전하게 (safely) |
| Special form | 많다 (many) | 많이 (a lot) |
| Special form | 빠르다 (fast) | 빨리 (quickly) |

**Example sentences:**
- 저는 조용하게 먹었어요 = I ate quietly
- 저는 거리를 안전하게 건넜어요 = I crossed the street safely
- 저는 행복하게 살았어요 = I lived happily

## Negative Sentences

There are two ways to make a sentence negative:

### Method 1: 안 (adverb)

Place **안** immediately before the final verb or adjective:

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 그 여자는 안 예뻐요 | That girl isn't pretty |
| 저는 생선을 안 좋아해요 | I don't like fish |
| 저는 내일 학교에 안 가겠어요 | I'm not going to school tomorrow |

### Method 2: ~지 않다 (verb)

Add **~지 않다** to the stem of the final verb/adjective:

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 그 여자는 예쁘지 않아요 | That girl isn't pretty |
| 저는 생선을 좋아하지 않아요 | I don't like fish |
| 저는 내일 학교에 가지 않겠어요 | I'm not going to school tomorrow |

> Both methods have identical meanings. Through exposure, you'll develop a preference for which to use in which situation.

### Notes on Negative Sentences with ~하다 Verbs

For verbs ending in ~하다, it is natural to separate ~하다 from the noun and place 안 between them:

| Correct | Less Natural |
|---------|-------------|
| 저는 공부를 **안** 했어요 | 저는 **안** 공부했어요 |
| 저는 실수를 **안** 했어요 | 저는 **안** 실수했어요 |
| 저는 여행을 **안** 했어요 | 저는 **안** 여행했어요 |

For ~하다 adjectives, you cannot separate ~하다. Place 안 before the word or use ~지 않다:

| Correct | |
|---------|---|
| 저는 안 행복해요 | I am not happy |
| 저는 행복하지 않아요 | I am not happy |

> You CANNOT say: 저는 행복을 안 해요 (incorrect)

## To not be: 아니다

**아니다** is the opposite of **이다**, but used differently. The particle **~이/가** is attached to the noun, and 아니다 is separate:

| Korean | Conjugated |
|--------|-----------|
| 나는 선생님이 아니다 | 저는 선생님이 아니에요 |
| 나는 너의 친구가 아니다 | 나는 너의 친구가 아니야 |
| 나는 대학생이 아니다 | 저는 대학생이 아니에요 |

## To not have: 없다

**없다** is the opposite of **있다**:

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 저는 돈이 없어요 | I don't have money |
| 저는 시간이 없어요 | I don't have time |
| 우리는 차가 없어요 | We don't have a car |
| 저의 친구는 지금 한국에 없어요 | My friend is not in Korea now |

## To not like: 싫어하다 and 싫다

| Word | Type | Usage |
|------|------|-------|
| 싫어하다 | verb | 싫어해요 (I dislike) — can act on object |
| 싫다 | adjective | 싫어요 (I dislike) — uses ~이/가 |

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 저는 과일을 싫어해요 | I dislike fruit (verb) |
| 저는 과일이 싫어요 | I dislike fruit (adjective) |

> Be careful not to make double negatives: 저는 과일을 싫어하지 않아요 = I don't dislike fruit (awkward)
