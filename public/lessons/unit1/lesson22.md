# Lesson 22: Subject-Object-Adjective Sentences and More on 이/가

Source: [How to Study Korean - Unit 1, Lesson 22](https://www.howtostudykorean.com/unit1/unit-1-lessons-17-25-2/lesson-22/)

## Subject-Object-Adjective Sentences

Adjectives and passive verbs cannot act on objects. Instead, ~이/가 is used instead of ~을/를:

| Sentence | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| 김치가 좋아요 | I like Kimchi |
| 학교가 싫어요 | I don't like school |
| 맥주가 좋아요 | I like beer |

**More examples:**
- 저는 한국이 좋아요 = I like Korea
- 저는 그 사람이 싫어요 = I don't like that person
- 저는 한국 음식이 그리워요 = I miss Korean food

> This structure uses ~이/가 on what would normally be the object

## 이/가 vs 는/은 (Deeper Understanding)

| Particle | Function |
|----------|----------|
| 는/은 | Topic marker, implies comparison |
| 이/가 | Subject marker, states a fact |

- 고양이**는** 집 뒤에 있다 = The cat is behind the house (compared to something else)
- 고양이**가** 집 뒤에 있다 = The cat is behind the house (simply stating a fact)

**When to prefer 이/가:**
- After 있다/없다 (have/don't have)
- With 많다/적다 (many/few)
- In new/important information
- After demonstratives (이/그/저)

## ~이/가 있는/없다 (Have/Don't Have)

- 저는 펜이 있어요 = I have a pen
- 저는 시간이 없어요 = I don't have time
- 저는 질문이 있어요 = I have a question
