# Lesson 23: Counting and Number Usage in Context

Source: [How to Study Korean - Unit 1, Lesson 23](https://www.howtostudykorean.com/unit1/unit-1-lessons-17-25-2/lesson-23/)

## When to Use Which Number System

| Sino-Korean | Pure Korean |
|-------------|-------------|
| Money | Counting things/people |
| Minutes in time | Hours in time |
| Phone numbers | Age (under 50) |
| Months (names) | Months (counting with 달) |
| Measurements | Sequential actions |

## Counting in Context

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 펜 두 개를 샀어요 | I bought two pens |
| 사람 세 명이 왔어요 | three people came |
| 한국에 다섯 번 갔어요 | I went to Korea five times |
| 차 세 대가 있어요 | I have three cars |
| 맥주 두 잔 주세요 | Two glasses of beer, please |

## Using ~의 with Counters

The number-counter combination can precede the noun with ~의:
- 두 명의 사람 = two people
- 다섯 개의 펜 = five pens

> The form "사람 두 명" is more common than "두 명의 사람"

## ~번 (Times an action is done)

번 acts as an adverb (no particle needed):
- 저는 한국에 세 번 갔어요 = I went to Korea three times
- 저는 오늘 두 번 운동했어요 = I exercised twice today

## Age

Pure Korean numbers + 살:
- 저는 열여덟 살이에요 = I am 18 years old
- 몇 살이에요? = How old are you?

## 처음 and 마지막

| Korean | English |
|--------|---------|
| 처음 | first time/beginning |
| 마지막 | last (final in sequence) |

- 저는 어제 공항에 처음 갔어요 = I went to the airport for the first time
- 이것은 저의 마지막 기회예요 = This is my last chance
