# Lesson 19: Comparisons and Superlatives

Source: [How to Study Korean - Unit 1, Lesson 19](https://www.howtostudykorean.com/unit1/unit-1-lessons-17-25-2/lesson-19/)

## Comparisons with ~보다

~보다 means "than" and is used to compare:

- A는 B보다 + adjective = A is more [adjective] than B

**Examples:**
- 저는 남동생보다 키가 커요 = I am taller than my brother
- 택시는 버스보다 빨라요 = The taxi is faster than the bus
- 한국어는 영어보다 어려워요 = Korean is more difficult than English
- 겨울 방학이 여름 방학보다 길어요 = Winter vacation is longer than summer vacation

## Superlatives: 가장/제일

| Korean | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 가장 | most (formal) |
| 제일 | most (common) |

- 저는 수학을 제일 좋아해요 = I like math the most
- 빨간 사과는 가장 맛있어요 = Red apples are the most delicious

## Similarity/Difference/Sameness

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| A는 B와 비슷하다 | A is similar to B |
| A는 B와 다르다 | A is different from B |
| A는 B와 똑같다 | A is exactly the same as B |

- 저는 친구와 비슷해요 = I am similar to my friend
- 그 건물은 어제와 달라요 = That building is different from yesterday
