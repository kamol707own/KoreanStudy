## Conjugating with Honorifics

In Korean, you must use different conjugations depending on who you are speaking to. The different conjugations imply respect and politeness.

### Three Levels of Speech

| Level | Name | Usage |
|-------|------|-------|
| 1 | Informal low respect | Friends, close people, younger people, family |
| 2 | Informal high respect | Most situations, showing respect |
| 3 | Formal high respect | People who deserve a lot of respect |

> Remember the ~아/어 rule: If the last vowel is ㅏ or ㅗ → add 아. If anything else → add 어. If 하 → add 해.

## Verbs

### Present Tense

| Level | Rule | Example (먹다) |
|-------|------|---------------|
| Informal low | Add ~어/아/여 | 먹어 |
| Informal high | Add ~어/아/여 + 요 | 먹어요 |
| Formal high | Add ~ㅂ니다/습니다 | 먹습니다 |

### Past Tense

| Level | Rule | Example (먹다) |
|-------|------|---------------|
| Informal low | Remove 다, add 어 after 었 | 먹었어 |
| Informal high | Add 요 | 먹었어요 |
| Formal high | Remove 다, add 습니다 after 었 | 먹었습니다 |

### Future Tense

| Level | Rule | Example (먹다) |
|-------|------|---------------|
| Informal low | Remove 다, add 어 after 겠 | 먹겠어 |
| Informal high | Add 요 | 먹겠어요 |
| Formal high | Remove 다, add 습니다 after 겠 | 먹겠습니다 |
