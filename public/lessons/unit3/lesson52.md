## Quoting with Verbs: ~ㄴ/는다고

In quoted sentences, the actual "quoted" part gets conjugated into the plain (or "diary") form, followed by "고."

| Verb | Past | Present | Future |
|------|------|---------|--------|
| 하다 | 했다 | 한다 | 하겠다 |
| 가다 | 갔다 | 간다 | 가겠다 |
| 먹다 | 먹었다 | 먹는다 | 먹겠다 |

Examples:
- 나는 그가 너를 좋아한다고 말했어 = I said that he likes you
- 나는 그가 너를 좋아했다고 말했어 = I said that he liked you
- 나는 그가 너를 좋아하겠다고 말했어 = I said that he will like you

You can indicate to whom the quote is said by attaching ~에게/한테/께:
- 나는 엄마에게 그가 너를 좋아한다고 말할 거야 = I will tell mom that he likes you

Korean people love shortening their sentences. Because ~ㄴ/는다고 already indicates that the sentence is quoted, you can just say "하다" instead of "말하다":
- 그는 공부하지 않았다고 했어요 = He said that he didn't study

## Quoting with Adjectives: ~다고

The plain form conjugation of adjectives in the present tense is the same as the dictionary form.

Examples:
- 밧줄의 길이가 너무 부족하다고 했어요 = I said that the length of this rope is not enough
- 아빠가 오늘 잡은 물고기가 너무 조그맣다고 했어요 = Dad said that the fish he caught today is very tiny

Remember that 있다 and 없다 are adjectives when indicating possession:
- 학생이 이해할 수 없다고 했어요 = The student said he can't understand
- 저는 현금이 없다고 해서 현금인출기에 갔어요 = I said that I don't have cash, so we went to the ATM

## Quoting with 이다: ~(이)라고

When quoting 이다 in the present tense, 라 replaces 다:

When the noun ends in a vowel, 이 can be omitted:
- 그가 의사이라고 했어요 = He said he is a doctor
- 그가 의사라고 했어요 = He said he is a doctor

When the noun ends in a consonant, 이 cannot be omitted:
- 우리 선생님이 제가 나쁜 학생이라고 했어요 = Our teacher said I am a bad student

This applies to future tense clauses using ~ㄹ 것이다:
- 나는 밥을 먹을 거라고 했다 = I said that I will eat

---

## Other Uses of ~ㄴ/는다고

1. **To think: 생각하다**
   - 저는 그 여자가 별로 안 예쁘다고 생각해요 = I think that girl isn't that pretty

2. **To believe: 믿다**
   - 저는 우리 팀이 이길 거라고 믿어요 = I believe that our team will win

3. **To call something: 부르다**
   - 사람들은 그 건물을 한국타워라고 불러요 = People call that building "Korea tower"

4. **To promise: 약속하다**
   - 현금을 내일 주겠다고 약속했다 = I promised to give you the cash tomorrow

## Asking Questions with Quoted Sentences

- 뭐라고? = What did you say?
- 뭐라고요? = What did you say? (more formal)
- 아빠가 뭐라고 말했어요? = What did dad say?

You can ask for clarification:
- Person 1: 밥을 먹기 싫어 = I don't want to eat
- Person 2: 밥을 먹기 싫다고? = You said that you don't want to eat?

---

## Using Quoted Sentences with ~는 것

간다는 것 is an abbreviation of 간다고 하는 것.

Examples:
- 그 여자가 다른 남자랑 애기가 있다는 소문이 있습니다 = There is a rumor that that woman has a baby with another man
- 그녀가 시험에 떨어졌다는 소문을 들었어요 = I heard a rumor that she failed the test
