## Introduction

In this lesson, you will learn about two opposite words: **포함하다** (to include something) and **제외하다** (to exclude something).

---

## To Include: 포함하다

**포함하다** can be placed at the end of a sentence to indicate that something "includes" something.

- 이 뷔페가격은 포도주를 포함해요 = This buffet price includes wine

It usually sounds more natural to use **포함되다** (passive):
- 이 뷔페가격은 포도주가 포함돼요 = This buffet price includes wine
- 나의 월세는 가스비가 포함된다 = My rent includes the gas bill
- 이 조명 가격은 세금이 포함돼요 = The price of these lights includes tax

**Usage in the middle of sentences:**

Attach **~아/어(서)** to 포함하다 to indicate what is included:
- 저를 포함해(서)… = including me…
- 세금을 포함해(서)… = including tax

**Example sentences:**
- 저를 포함해 여기에 네 명이 있어요 = Including me, there are four people here
- 세금을 포함해 이 집은 20만 원입니다 = Including taxes, this house is 200,000 won
- 저의 친구를 포함해 열 명이 같이 여행할 거예요 = Including my friend, ten people will go travel together
- 구급차 비용을 포함해 치료비가 100만 원이었어요 = Including the ambulance fee, the treatment was 1,000,000 won
- 다리에 있는 문신을 포함해서 저는 문신 네 개가 있어요 = Including the tattoo on my leg, I have four tattoos

> You can also attach **~(으)면** to 포함하다 instead of ~아/어(서):
> - 저를 포함하면 여기에 네 명이 있어요 = If you include me, there are four people here

## To exclude: 제외하다

**제외하다** can be placed at the end of a sentence:
- 나쁜 성적 때문에 선생님은 그 학생을 여행에서 제외했어요 = The teacher excluded the student from the trip because of his bad grades

**Usage in the middle of sentences:**

Place **제외하고** or **제외한** after the noun you want to exclude:
- 저의 어머니를 제외하고… = Except for my mother…
- 저의 어머니를 제외한… = Except for my mother…

> **Note:** While ~아/어(서) is used with 포함하다, it is **not** added to 제외하다. Instead, use ~ㄴ or ~고 with 제외하다.

**With 제외하고:**
- 나비를 제외하고 모든 동물이 싫어요 = Except for butterflies, I don't like any animals
- 하체를 제외하고 온몸을 운동했어요 = Except for my lower body, I exercised my whole body
- 저는 축구를 제외하고 모든 스포츠가 좋아요 = I like all sports except for soccer
- 일요일을 제외하고 저는 매일 일해요 = Except for Sunday, I work every day

**With 제외한:**
- 열 명을 제외한 사람들이 다 죽었어요 = Except for ten people, everybody died

> **Tip:** When the noun being described by 제외한 is also described by another verb, use 제외하고 instead. When excluding adverbs (place, time, day), use 제외하고.

**Common alternative: 빼다**

"빼다" (to take out) is often used instead of 제외하다:
- 나비를 빼고 모든 동물이 싫어요
- 햄을 빼고 주세요 = Please hold the ham
- 햄 빼고요! = No ham (please)
- 커피에 우유 빼고요 = Please don't put milk in my coffee

## Outside of: 외에

The word **"외"** (Hanja: 外) means "outside." Placing 외에 after a noun creates the meaning of "outside of" or "other than."

- 그 선생님 외에 이 일을 할 수 있는 사람이 없어요 = Outside of that teacher, there is nobody who can do this job
- 저는 음식 외에 홍삼도 샀어요 = Other than food, I also bought Ginseng
- 조명 외에 또 고쳐야 되는 것이 있나요? = Other than the lighting, is there anything else that needs to be fixed?
- 한국 사람들과 얘기하는 것 외에 저는 다른 공부를 하지 않았어요 = Outside of speaking with Korean people, I didn't do any other studying
