## Perhaps: 어쩌면

어쩌면 is often used when the speaker is making a guess about a situation. Often paired with ~ㄹ/을지(도) 모르다:

- 어쩌면 우리가 내일 갈 수 있을지도 몰라요 = Perhaps we will be able to go tomorrow
- 어쩌면 그녀의 남자 친구가 그녀를 위해 선물을 살지 몰라요 = Perhaps her boyfriend will buy her a present

어쩌면 can also stress amazement:
- 어쩌면 이렇게 일찍 왔어요? = How is it possible that you came this early?
- 어쩌면 비가 저렇게 많이 왔어요? = How is it possible that it rained that much?

---

## Probably: 아무래도

아무래도 is often used when the speaker thinks something will occur. Often paired with 것 같다:

- 아무래도 그녀가 우리를 만나러 안 올 것 같아요 = She probably won't come to meet us
- 아무래도 그가 벌써 떠났을 것 같아요 = He probably already left

---

## To grow/To make look pretty: 가꾸다

**Growing/cultivating:**
- 나이가 먹으면 정원을 가꾸는 것은 좋은 운동이에요 = When you get older, gardening is good exercise
- 우리는 옛날에 우리 집에서 채소를 가꿨어요 = A long time ago we grew vegetables at our house

**Making oneself look nice:**
- 제가 소개팅을 하기 전에 외모를 가꿔야 돼요 = Before I go on a blind date, I need to fix myself up
- 여자들이 파티에 가기 전에 예쁘게 가꿨어요 = Before the girls went to the party, they dolled themselves up

---

## Age: 연세

연세 is the formal equivalent of "나이." Use it when talking to much older people (roughly 65+).

- 연세가 어떻게 되세요? = How old are you?
- 할아버지가 연세가 많아서 걷기가 힘들어요 = Grandpa is old, so it is difficult for him to walk

---

## To see: 뵈다

뵈다 is the honorific equivalent of "보다" (to see):

- 저는 어제 선생님의 할아버지를 뵈었어요 = I saw your grandpa yesterday

The conjugation is the same as 되다:
- 뵈어 / 봬 (both acceptable)
- 뵈었어 / 봤어 (both acceptable)

> **Note:** 뵙다 is slightly more formal. It can only be used when followed by a consonant:
- 내일 뵙겠습니다 = See you tomorrow

---

## Wearing…: 차림으로

차림 refers to one's clothes/outfit:
- 옷차림 = outfit
- 잠옷 차림 = pajamas
- 교복 차림 = school uniform
- 정장 차림 = a suit
- 운동복 차림 = exercise clothes

~(으)로 indicates doing an action while wearing:
- 그는 잠옷 차림으로 밖에 나갔어요 = He went outside wearing his pajamas
- 저는 매일 교복 차림으로 학교에 갔어요 = I went to school every day wearing my uniform

---

## In other words: 즉

즉 means "in other words" or "i.e.":

- 그녀는 자기 남자 친구에게 그녀의 집에서 나가라고 했어요. 즉 그들은 헤어졌어요. = She told her boyfriend to get out of her house. In other words, they broke up
- 엄마가 전화를 갑자기 끊었어요. 즉 저랑 통화하기 싫어하는 것 같아요 = Mom suddenly hung up the phone. In other words, it seems that she doesn't want to talk with me
