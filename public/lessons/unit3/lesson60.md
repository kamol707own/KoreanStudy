# Lesson 60: Difficult words: 차다, 가득, 왠지, 설마, 찍다, 다행, ~달

## To be full: 가득 차다

채우다 is active "to fill." 차다 is passive "to be filled/full."

- 교실이 학생들로 완전히 찼어요 = The classroom is completely filled with students
- 식당이 손님으로 완전히 차 있어요 = The restaurant is completely full with customers

가득 is an adverb meaning "completely/filled":
- 교실은 학생들로 가득 찼어요 = The classroom is completely filled with students

차다 can also mean "full" with emotions:
- 자신감에 찬 표정으로 새로운 직장에 들어갔어요 = He went into a new workplace with a face full of confidence
- 시험결과를 보고 저는 기쁨으로 가득 찼어요 = After looking at the exam results, I was full of joy

---

## For some reason: 왠지

왠지 has the meaning of "왜 그런지 몰라요" (I don't know why):

- 그녀는 왠지 한국어를 배우고 싶었어요 = She wanted to learn Korean for some reason
- 나는 왠지 오늘 학교에 가기 싫어 = I don't want to go to school today for some reason

---

## Expressing Astonishment: 설마

설마 expresses amazement or astonishment:
- 설마 밥을 다 먹었어요? = Don't tell me you ate all the rice?!
- 설마 아직도 아파요? = Don't tell me you are still sick?
- 설마 그곳에 혼자 가요? = Don't tell me you are going there by yourself?
- 설마 우리가 내일 학교에 가야 돼요? = Don't tell me that we have to go to school tomorrow?

---

## To take a picture and other usages: 찍다

찍다 has many meanings centered around touching one thing to another:

- **Taking a picture:** 저는 사진을 찍었어요 = I took a picture
- **Stamping:** 저는 편지에 도장을 찍었어요 = I stamped a stamp on a letter
- **Dipping:** 고기를 소스에 찍어야 돼요 = You need to dip the meat in the sauce
- **Putting a period:** 문장을 다 쓰면 점을 찍어야 돼요 = When you are done writing a sentence, you need to put a period
- **Scanning:** 카드를 거기에 찍어야 돼요 = You need to scan your card there

---

## Thankfully: 다행(이다)

다행하다 describes a fortunate or lucky event:
- 아무도 안 죽은 것은 다행한 일이에요 = It is fortunate nobody died

다행 is often used after ~아/어서 to express thankfulness:
- 비가 안 와서 다행이에요 = Thankfully it didn't rain
- 물이 별로 안 뜨거워서 다행이에요 = Thankfully the water isn't too hot
- 사진을 많이 찍어서 다행이에요 = Thankfully you took many pictures
- 아직 사람이 별로 없어서 다행이에요 = Thankfully there aren't that many people yet

---

## At some point in a month: ~달

Attach 달 to a month name for "some time in ___":
- 저는 5월달에 한국에 갈 거예요 = I'm going to Korea sometime in May
- 저는 6월달에 시험을 볼 거예요 = I'm going to take an exam sometime in June
