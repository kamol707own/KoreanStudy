## Introduction

In this lesson, you will learn how to apply **~아/어(서)** to sentences. Explaining this concept is probably the most difficult of all grammatical forms, simply because the meaning is so subtle.

---

## An Intrinsic Temporal Relationship: ~아/어(서)

When **~아/어(서)** is used to connect two clauses, the action in the first clause and the action in the second clause occur in the order in which they are said. This is the same as when **~고** is used, but there doesn't need to be a direct connection between the actions when ~고 is used.

When you use **~아/어(서)**, however, there is more likely to be an intrinsic relationship between the two clauses — not only did the second action happen after the first action, but the second action happened because the first action allowed it to.

**Examples:**
- 저는 학교에 가서 공부할 거예요 = I will go to school and then study
- 우리는 집에 와서 바로 잤어요 = We came home and went to sleep immediately

**Examples with ~아/어 and ~아/어서 (identical meaning):**
- 새로운 컴퓨터를 사 작업을 했어요 / 새로운 컴퓨터를 사서 작업을 했어요 = I bought a new computer, and then did my work
- 케이크를 만들어 친구들에게 나눠 줬어요 / 케이크를 만들어서 친구들에게 나눠 줬어요 = I made a cake, and then gave it out to my friends
- 서랍을 열어 돈을 찾았어요 / 서랍을 열어서 돈을 찾았어요 = I opened the drawer and then found the money

> In all of the cases above, **~고** can replace **~아/어(서)**. You are more likely to find ~아/어(서) being used if you feel there is an intrinsic connection between the two clauses.

---

## An Action Happens Through the Means of Another Action: ~아/어서

**~아/어서** can be attached to a verb to indicate "how" or "through what means" a second action takes place.

- 저는 걸어서 집에 갔어요 = I went home by walking (I walked home)

> **Important:** In these types of sentences where ~아/어서 is being used to indicate a "method" or "means," "서" is **not optional**.

**Examples:**
- 슬기가 몸을 쭈그려서 예쁜 꽃의 향기를 맡아봤어요 = Seulgi crouched over and smelled the beautiful flowers
- 제가 당신을 위해 정성 들여서 만든 음식입니다 = This is a food that I made for you by putting all of my love/effort into it
- 대통령이 연설을 해서 청중을 사로잡았다 = The president captivated the audience through/with a speech
- 저는 전력을 다해서 이 일을 내일까지 마무리해 볼 거예요 = I will use all of my power and try to finish this work by tomorrow
- 지구본을 사용해서 지도를 그렸어요 = He used the globe to draw a map
- 우리는 힘을 다 합해서 일을 마무리했어요 = We put together all of our efforts and finished that job

> **Note:** There is a lot of overlap between the usages of ~아/어서 and ~아/어(서). The meaning is often up to the speaker to make clear or for the listener to interpret.

---

## Used to combine the meanings of two words: ~아/어

You will often see two words combined with **~아/어** to combine their meanings. When used like this, "서" is not attached.

**Examples of combined verbs:**
- 걸어가다 = to go by walking (걷다 + 가다)
- 걸어오다 = to come by walking (걷다 + 오다)
- 뛰어다니다 = to run around an area (뛰다 + 다니다)
- 돌아보다 = to turn and look (돌다 + 보다)
- 알려주다 = to let somebody know (알리다 + 주다)
- 지켜보다 = to protect by watching (지키다 + 보다)
- 비켜서다 = to stand/step aside (비키다 + 서다)
- 올려놓다 = to place something on something (올리다 + 놓다)
- 뛰어가다 = to run to (뛰다 + 가다)
- 달려가다 = to run to (달리다 + 가다)
- 뛰어오다 = to come by running (뛰다 + 오다)
- 달려오다 = to come by running (달리다 + 오다)

**Example sentences:**
- 아이들이 놀이터에서 뛰어다녔어요 = The kids were running around the playground
- 방에 들어가서 책을 책상에 올려놓았어요 = I went into the room and put my book on the desk
- 너무 늦어서 학교에 뛰어갔어요 = I was very late, so I ran to school
- 너무 늦어서 학교에 달려갔어요 = I was very late, so I ran to school

**Two separate verbs combined with ~아/어 (with space):**
- 걸어 다니다 = to walk around an area
- 돌려 보내다 = to send something back
- 뛰어 넘다 = to run/jump over something
- 꺼내 놓다 = to take out and put down
- 아껴 쓰다 = to save something
- 걷어 올리다 = to tuck up one's sleeves/pants

## The many meanings of 통하다

The word **"통하다"** has many meanings depending on the situation. Most of these meanings relate to two things being connected, and there being a passageway or flow between them.

- 이 방은 공기가 잘 안 통해요 = Air doesn't flow well in this room
- 이 식당에 옆 식당과 통하는 길이 있어요 = There is a road that connects this restaurant with the restaurant next door
- 그 사람과 말이 안 통해서 그 사람을 이해할 수 없어요 = I couldn't understand that person
- 한국에서 그 여자가 제일 예쁜 가수로 통한다 = That girl is known as the prettiest singer in Korea

**통해(서) meaning "through":**

- 불이 나고 문이 막혀 있어서 창문을 통해 빠져나갔어요 = I escaped through the window
- 교과서를 통해서 그 사실을 배웠다 = I learned that fact through that book
- 친구를 통해 소문을 들었어요 = I heard a rumor from/through my friend
- 저는 저의 여자 친구를 통해 한국말을 배웠습니다 = I learned Korean through my girlfriend

## To hurry: 서두러

The verb **"서두르다"** means "to hurry" and can be used as a stand-alone verb or before another verb connected with **~아/어(서)**.

- 우리는 서둘러 나갔어요 = We hurried outside
- 선생님은 서둘러 학생들에게 수업을 가르쳤요 = The teacher hurried to teach his students the class
