## Introduction

In this lesson, you will learn how to use **만큼** and **정도**, which can be used to create the translation "as much as…"

---

## As much as: ~만큼

**~만큼** can be attached directly to a noun to describe that something is done to the extent as that noun.

**Common constructions:**
- 나만큼 = as much as me
- 밥만큼 = as much as rice
- 남자 배우만큼 = as much as actors
- 한국 사람만큼 = as much as a Korean person
- 너만큼 = as much as you

**Example sentences:**
- 그는 나만큼 축구를 잘해 = He plays soccer as well as me
- 과일은 밥만큼 건강에 좋아요 = Fruit is as healthy as rice is
- 여배우들은 남자 배우만큼 돈을 못 벌어요 = Actresses don't make as much as actors do
- 저는 한국 사람만큼 한국말을 할 수 있어요 = I can speak Korean as much as a Korean person can

**With verbs (using ~는 것):**
- 고기를 먹는 것만큼 야채를 먹어야 한다 = One should eat vegetables as much as they eat meat
- 게임을 하는 것만큼 일을 열심히 하면 성공할 수 있다 = If you work as much as you play games, you can succeed
- 바람이 많이 부는 날에 연을 날리는 것만큼 재미있는 게 없어요 = On really windy days, there is nothing as fun as flying a kite

**With clauses describing 만큼:**
- 우리가 이 파티를 즐긴 만큼 너도 즐겼으면 좋겠어 = I hope you enjoyed the party to the extent that we enjoyed it
- 네가 원하는 만큼 먹어 = Eat to the extent that you want

> **Note:** Korean people can't distinguish between ~는 만큼 and ~는 것만큼 when the describing clause is in the past or present tense.

**With future tense (hyperbole):**
- 죽을 만큼 너를 사랑해 = I love you to the point that I am going to die
- 밥도 못 살 만큼 가난해요 = He is so poor he can't even buy rice
- 길에서 넘어질 만큼 깜짝 놀랐어요 = I was so surprised that I could have fallen
- 배꼽이 빠질 만큼 웃었어요 = I laughed so much my belly button could have exploded
- 오늘 밤에 간 이식을 받아야 될 만큼 술을 마실 거예요 = Tonight, I'm going to drink so much that I'm going to need a liver transplant

**Idioms with 만큼:**
- **눈곱만큼도** = not in the slightest bit
  - 나는 너를 눈곱만큼도 좋아하지 않아 = I don't like you in the slightest bit
  - 그런 식료품에 영양소가 눈곱만큼도 없어요 = There isn't the slightest bit of nutrients in that type of food

- **하늘만큼 땅만큼** = "I love you thiiiiiiiiiiiiis much" (from earth to sky)

## Degree: 정도

**정도** means "degree" and can replace **만큼** in sentences where it is not attached directly to a noun.

- 죽을 정도 = to the degree that one will die
- 학교에 안 갈 정도 = to the degree that one won't go to school

**~로 is often added to 정도:**
- 나는 죽을 정도로 너를 사랑해 = I love you to the degree that I will die
- 그는 학교에 못 갈 정도로 아파요 = He is sick to the degree that he won't go to school

**Examples:**
- 밥도 못 살 정도로 가난해요 = He is so poor to the degree that he can't even buy rice
- 길에서 넘어질 정도로 깜짝 놀랐어요 = I was so surprised that I could have fallen on the street
- 배꼽이 빠질 정도로 웃었어요 = I laughed so much my belly button could have exploded

**With other particles:**
- 돈이 부족해서 밥도 못 살 정도의 사람들이 불쌍해요 = People who don't even have enough money to buy rice are pathetic
- 지금 월세도 못 낼 정도까지 돈이 없어요 = I don't have any money to the point that I can't even pay my rent

**As a rough estimate:**
- 우리는 아직 40분 정도 가야 돼요 = We still have to go about 40 minutes
- 사람 열 명 정도가 수업을 들을 거예요 = About 10 people will take the class
- 그 책은 만 원 정도입니다 = That book is about 10,000 won

## 어느 정도/만큼

**어느** can be used with 정도 and 만큼:

**As a question ("to what extent"):**
- 옆구리가 어느 정도 아파요? = How sore is your side?
- 옆구리가 얼마만큼 아파요? = How sore is your side?

> **Note:** "얼마만큼" is often used instead of "어느 만큼"

**As a statement ("to some extent"):**
- 반찬을 어느 정도 만들 수 있어요 = I can make 반찬 to some extent
- 그 사람이 무슨 말을 하고 있는지 어느 정도 이해해요 = I understand what he is trying to say to some extent
- 그 학생은 영어를 어느 정도 잘 할 수 있어요 = That student can speak English to a certain degree
