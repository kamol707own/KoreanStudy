## Introduction

In this lesson, you will learn another grammatical principle that can be translated to "only" — the particle **~밖에**.

---

## Nothing but/only: ~밖에

The word **"밖"** has the meaning "outside." It can be used as a place in a sentence to refer to the outdoors, or to describe that something is "outside" of a specific area.

When **~밖에** is attached to a noun as a particle, the speaker indicates that only that noun — and nothing but that noun — is acted on. The common translation is "nothing but."

**Examples:**
- 친구밖에… = nothing but friend(s)
- 밥밖에… = nothing but rice
- 만 원밖에… = nothing but 10,000 won

This is similar to the particle **~만**, which simply means "only." However, when ~밖에 is used, the speaker is emphasizing that "nothing but" a particular noun is acted on.

> **Important:** When ~밖에 is attached to a noun to have this meaning, the final verb should be a **negative word**. It is common for these sentences to end in 없다.

**Examples:**
- 저는 친구밖에 없어요 = I have nothing but friends
- 우리가 먹을 것은 밥밖에 없어요 = We have nothing but rice to eat
- 유행을 따라가는 사람들이 청소년들밖에 없어요 = The only people who follow trends are young people
- 그거를 사고 싶지만 만 원밖에 없어서 못 사요 = I want to buy that, but I only have 10,000 won, so I can't buy it

> **Note:** You can see the similarity with the meaning of "outside" if you translate slightly differently:
> - 저는 친구밖에 없어요 = Outside of friends, I have nothing

**With counters or 하나:**
- 저는 친구가 세 명밖에 없어요 = I have nothing but three friends
- 저는 신용카드가 하나밖에 없어요 = I have nothing but one credit card

**With other verbs (not just 없다):**

Even though the (noun)밖에 construction translates to "nothing but (noun)," the sentence should end with a negative conjugation. This often makes it look like there is a double negative for English speakers.

- 코치가 우리 팀에게 충고를 하나밖에 안 했었어요 = The coach gave us nothing but one piece of advice
- 남자 친구가 너한테 돈밖에 안 줬다고? = Your boyfriend gave you nothing but money?
- 아빠가 배추를 하나밖에 주문하지 않았어요 = Dad ordered nothing but one (head of) cabbage
- 제가 아는 공연 종류는 연극밖에 없어요 = The only type of performance I know about is plays
- 선생님! 저에게 답안지밖에 안 주셨어요 = Teacher! You only gave me an answer sheet
- 그 선생님의 수업을 한번밖에 참관하지 않았어요 = I only attended that teacher's class once
- 저는 평생 동안 오렌지를 세 번밖에 안 먹어 봤어요 = I have only eaten oranges three times in my life

**Emphasizing short time:**
- 30 분밖에 안 걸려요! = It only takes 30 minutes
- 한 시간밖에 안 걸려요 = It only takes 1 hour

## There is nothing I can do but…: ~ㄹ/을 수밖에 없다

In Lesson 45 you learned about the endings **~ㄹ/을 수 있다** and **~ㄹ/을 수 없다**. By attaching **~밖에** to 수 in "~할 수 없다" you describe that the best/only option is to do the specified action.

**Translation:** "to have no choice but to…"

**Examples:**
- 우리가 밥을 먹을 수밖에 없어요 = We have no choice but to eat
- 우리가 연극을 다른 데에서 할 수밖에 없어요 = We have no choice but to do the play in a different place
- 죄송하지만 저는 당신을 해고할 수밖에 없어요 = I'm sorry, but I can't do anything but fire you
- 쓰레기가 많아서 방을 싹 청소할 수밖에 없어요 = I have no choice but to completely clean the room
- 일이 너무 심심해서 다른 분야로 옮길 수밖에 없어요 = I have no choice but to move/switch fields
- 시험을 네 번 연속으로 못 봐서 포기할 수밖에 없어요 = I have no choice but to give up
- 내일도 비가 올 거라서 연극을 또 취소할 수밖에 없어요 = It will rain again tomorrow, so we have no choice but to cancel the play
- 친구가 늦게 올 거라고 해서 우리는 기다릴 수밖에 없어요 = Our friend said he will come late, so we have nothing to do but wait
- 내일 해변에 갈 거라서 겨드랑이 털을 면도할 수밖에 없어요 = I am going to the beach tomorrow, so I have no choice but to shave my armpits
- 오랫동안 운동해서 에너지를 다 소모해서 밥을 많이 먹을 수밖에 없어요 = I exercised for a long time and used up all of my energy, so I have no choice but to eat a lot of food
