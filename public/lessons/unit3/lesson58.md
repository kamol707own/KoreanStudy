## (Noun) or (Noun): ~(이)나

~(이)나 indicates that it hasn't been decided which noun/object will be acted on:

- 저는 빵이나 밥을 먹고 싶어요 = I want to eat bread or rice
- 저는 술이나 콜라를 마시고 싶어요 = I want to drink alcohol or cola
- 딸이나 아들을 낳고 싶어요? = Do you want to have a daughter or a son?
- 대학교에서 철학이나 화학을 공부하고 싶어요? = In University, do you want to study philosophy or chemistry?

When attached to one word without a second option, it expresses a last resort:
- 밥이나 먹을래? = Well… shall we just eat rice?
- 영화나 보자 = (Well…) Let's go see a movie
- 운동이나 할래? = (Well…) Shall we exercise?

This also applies to words from Lesson 25:
- 아무 거나 = anything
- 아무 데나 = anywhere
- 아무 때나 = anytime

## (Verb) or (Verb): ~거나

~거나 is attached to verbs to indicate that it hasn't been decided which action will be completed:

- 여자 친구를 위해 편지를 쓰거나 선물을 사 줄 거예요 = I will write a letter, or buy a present for my girlfriend
- 신발끈을 묶거나 신발을 벗으세요 = Tie your shoes or take them off
- 오래된 차를 고치거나 새로운 차를 사야 돼요 = I need to fix my old car or buy a new one

~거나 can also be used with adjectives:
- 이 문제가 너무 어렵거나 도움이 필요하면 교무실로 오세요 = If this question is too difficult or you need help, come to the office

~거나 can be used twice in a sentence to discuss the choice itself:
- 비가 오거나 안 오거나 중요하지 않아요 = It's not important if it rains or not
- 나머지를 먹거나 버리거나 결정해야 돼요 = You need to decide if you're going to eat the rest or throw it out

~거나 말거나 means "regardless of":
- 대학교에 가거나 말거나 공부를 해야 돼요 = You need to study if you go to University or not
- 네가 나를 사랑하거나 말거나 나는 떠날 거야 = Regardless of if you love me or not, I'm leaving
