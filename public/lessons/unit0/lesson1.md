# Korean Word Structure and Basic Letters

Source: [How to Study Korean - Unit 0, Lesson 1](https://www.howtostudykorean.com/unit0/unit0lesson1/)

For now, don't even think about words or grammar or anything until you can read and pronounce Korean letters and syllables. Without being able to read Korean, it is very difficult to continue studying other parts of the language.

In the Unit 0 lessons I will provide the Romanized equivalents to the Korean alphabet. However, I highly suggest that once you know how to read the Korean alphabet, you should completely abandon the Romanizations. For example, in the future, instead of studying like this:

학교 (hak-kyo) = school

You should study like this:

학교 = school

Study these characters like crazy. Memorizing them at first is hard, but it needs to be done. Luckily, Korean has a fairly simple 'alphabet', although it seems strange to most English speakers at first because it is completely different than English.

> Note: The letters taught in Unit 0 are not in alphabetical order. Rather, the most simple letters are presented first, and more complex letters later.

## Basic Vowels

| Korean | Romanization | Orientation |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| ㅣ | i | Vertical |
| ㅏ | a | Vertical |
| ㅓ | eo | Vertical (sounds like "uh") |
| ㅡ | eu | Horizontal |
| ㅜ | u | Horizontal |
| ㅗ | o | Horizontal |

> The first three vowels are drawn vertically, and the bottom three are drawn horizontally. The difference is very important because the way every Korean letter is written depends on if the vowel is drawn vertically or horizontally.

## Korean Syllable Blocks

Korean is written into "blocks" that make up one syllable. One block always has exactly one syllable. The blocks are ALWAYS drawn in one of the following ways:

**Important rules:**
1. Number "2" is ALWAYS a vowel
2. Numbers "1, 3" (and sometimes 4) are ALWAYS consonants
3. Blocks containing a horizontally drawn vowel use a specific structure
4. Blocks containing a vertically drawn vowel use a different structure

### Example: Writing "bab"

- Step 1: Determine if the vowel is horizontal or vertical. **ㅏ** is vertical
- Step 2: Determine if the syllable ends in a consonant. Yes → need positions 1, 2, and 3
- Step 3: Place **ㅂ** (b) in position 1, **ㅏ** (a) in position 2, **ㅂ** (b) in position 3
