## Introduction

In this lesson, you will learn about words that contain an extra ㅅ. This is called a 사이시옷.

## 사이시옷: Putting ㅅ Within Compound Words

When two individual words get combined to make a compound word, and the final syllable of the first part doesn't have a 받침 (final consonant), ㅅ is often inserted.

Example: 어제 + 밤 → **어젯밤** (last night)

## Rules

1. **사이시옷 is only added between constituents of a compound word**, not within a regular word
2. **Only added when the first part doesn't have a 받침**: 깻잎 (깻 has no 받침) vs 감잎 (감 has 받침, so no 사이시옷)
3. **Never added with foreign loan-words**: 피자집 (피자 is from English)
4. **At least one constituent must be of pure Korean origin**: 뱃길 (배, 비, 길 are Korean) vs 등굣길 (등교 is Hanja, 길 is Korean)
5. **Exceptions**: 숫자, 횟수, 찻간, 곳간, 셋방, 툇간

## Pronunciation Rules

**Rule 1:** ㅅ is not pronounced, and the following consonant is doubled:
- 만둣국 → 만두꾹
- 뱃길 → 배낄
- 찻집 → 차찝
- 햇빛 → 해삗
- 바닷가 → 바다까

**Rule 2:** If the first sound after 사이시옷 is ㄴ or ㅁ, ㅅ changes to ㄴ:
- 빗물 → 빈물
- 시냇물 → 시내물
- 훗날 → 훈날
- 뒷면 → 뒨면

**Rule 3:** If the first sound after 사이시옷 is a vowel, ㅅ changes to ㄴ and ㄴ replaces ㅇ:
- 깻잎 → 깬닛
- 나뭇잎 → 나문닛
- 배춧잎 → 배춘닛
