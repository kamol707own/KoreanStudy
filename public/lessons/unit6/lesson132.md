## Introduction

In this lesson, you will learn how to attach ~ㄹ/을수록 to the end of a clause. It indicates that as one situation changes to some degree, another situation is affected and also changes to that degree.

## As One Situation Changes, Another Situation Changes: ~ㄹ/을수록

A common translation is "The more... the more..." or "As..."

### Example Sentences
- 공부를 할수록 똑똑해요 = The more you study, the smarter you get
- 발톱이 길어질수록 아파요 = The longer my toenails get, the more they hurt
- 어두워질수록 야경이 예뻐요 = As it gets darker, the night view gets prettier
- 가방에 물건을 넣을수록 무거워져요 = As you put more things in the bag, the heavier it gets
- 휘발유가 비쌀수록 교통비가 비싸져요 = The more expensive gasoline is, the more expensive transportation costs will be
- 스트레스를 받을수록 혈압이 높아져요 = The more stressed you are, the more your blood pressure goes up
- 사람들이 돈이 많을수록 욕심이 많아요 = The more money people have, the greedier they are
- 시간이 늦을수록 버스 운행 간격이 줄어요 = As it gets later, the interval time for buses decreases
- 애기가 피곤할수록 애기를 재우기가 더 쉬워요 = The more tired the baby is, the easier it is to put him asleep
- 소리를 크게 지를수록 메아리가 더 크게 돌아와요 = The louder you scream, the louder the echo is
- 꿀벌이 없어질수록 인류에 영향을 미치는 문제가 많아요 = As bees disappear, there will be more problems that affect humans
- 오존층이 없어질수록 지구에 들어오는 자외선이 많아져요 = The more the ozone layer disappears, the more UV rays enter Earth
