## Introduction

~기에 and ~길래 are used to create a meaning of "so" or "because." ~길래 is the more colloquial form of ~기에.

## ~기에 and ~길래

The key distinction: ~길래 is placed on a verb or adjective that one **directly experiences** (by doing, seeing or hearing). That experience then leads the speaker to do a particular action or think a particular way.

### Examples
- 소고기가 마트에서 할인하길래 많이 사 왔어요 = Beef was on sale at the supermarket, so I bought a lot of it
- 강아지가 아주 순해 보이길래 잠깐 쓰다듬었어요 = The puppy looked so tame, so I pet it for a short time
- 제 친구가 시합에서 우승을 했길래 친구를 축하해 줬어요 = My friend won the championship, so I congratulated him
- 요즘에는 일교차가 심하길래 항상 겉옷을 가지고 다녀요 = These days, the daily temperature difference is severe, so I always bring my outerwear
- 자연분만이 제왕절개보다 회복이 빠르길래 이번에도 자연분만하기로 했어요 = Natural birth recovery is faster than C-section, so I decided to give birth naturally again

### At the end of a sentence (response to "why")
- 왜 그 식당에서 먹었어요? = Why did you eat at that restaurant?
- 친구가 자꾸 강추했길래… = Because a friend kept recommending it…
