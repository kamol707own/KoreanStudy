## Introduction

This lesson teaches how to end sentences with a bit of attitude using ~ㄴ/는다니, ~다니, ~(이)라니, ~자니, ~(느)냐니, and ~(으)라니.

## Responding in Surprise or Displeasure: ~ㄴ/는다니

Adding ~ㄴ/는다니 to verbs (or ~다니 to adjectives) expresses surprise, displeasure, or astonishment to what was said before.

### With Verbs
- 지금 간다니? = You're going now? You just got here!
- 지금 먹는다니? = You're eating now? It's 1:00am!
- 좌절했다니? = Frustrated? There aren't many people who pass that test on the first try

### With Adjectives
- 민망하다니? = Embarrassed?
- 넉넉하다니? = Enough? There is so little I'm going to have to start drinking sea water soon
- 못마땅하다니? = Aren't happy? Who could possibly be better than your boyfriend?

### ~라니 with Nouns
- 다용도실이라니?! = Utility room?! If you put it there, anybody could just take it
- 대머리라니? = Bald?
- 30살이라니? 지금 45살이야! = 30? He is 45 years old!

### ~자니 (response to a suggestion)
- 지금 나가자니? 날씨가 너무 추워 = Let's go outside, you say? The weather is so cold

### ~(느)냐니 (response to a question)
- 내가 포기했냐니? 새벽부터 이 문제를 고치려고 일하고 있었어 = Did you ask if I gave up? I was working since sunrise

### ~(으)라니 (response to a command)
- 모자를 벗으라니? 내가 대머리인지 알지? = Did you tell me to take off this hat? Do you know that I'm bald?
