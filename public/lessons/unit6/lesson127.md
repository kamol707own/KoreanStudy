## Introduction

In this lesson, you will learn how to attach ~씩 to indicate that an object, time or action will be divided into a specific quantity or regular intervals.

## To Be Done in a Divisible Quantity: ~씩

~씩 is often attached to something that can be divided quantitatively:
- 한 개 = one thing → **한 개씩**
- 한 시간 = one hour → **한 시간씩**
- 한번 = one time → **한번씩**

~씩 indicates that the object, time or action will be divided into the quantity specified and then acted on by the predicating verb.

### Giving Items
- 저는 아이들에게 과자를 한 개씩 줄 거예요 = I will give one cookie to each kid
- 저는 아이들에게 과자를 두 개씩 줄 거예요 = I will give two cookies to each kid
- 저는 아이들에게 국을 한 그릇씩 줄 거예요 = I will give one bowl of soup to each kid

### Example Sentences
- 저는 자두를 나무에서 하나씩 땄어요 = I plucked the plums from the tree one at a time
- 건포도를 애기들에게 몇 개씩 주면 되죠? = How many raisins should I give to the babies?
- 우리는 모든 행성에 대해 하나씩 배울 거예요 = We will learn about all of the planets one by one
- 세탁기가 망가져서 셔츠를 하나씩 빨아야 돼요 = I need to wash my shirts one at a time because the laundry machine is broken

### Using ~씩 with Counted Actions
- 저는 한 달에 두 번씩 병원에 가야 돼요 = I need to go to the hospital twice a month
- 저는 매주 한 번씩 병원에서 봉사를 해요 = I volunteer once per week at the hospital
- 맥박을 쟀는데 맥박이 1초에 두 번씩 뛰어요 = I measured your pulse, and your heart is beating twice per second

### Using ~씩 with Time
- 학생들이 30분씩 운동해요 = Students exercise for 30 minutes
- 저는 영어를 하루에 두 시간씩 공부해요 = I study English 2 hours a day

### Using ~씩 with 조금 and 가끔
- 배가 아프니 밥을 조금씩 드세요 = Only eat a little bit of rice at a time because your stomach is sore
- 돈을 아르바이트로 조금씩 벌 수 있어요 = You can earn money little by little with a part-time job
- 저는 낮잠을 가끔씩 자요 = I take naps sometimes
