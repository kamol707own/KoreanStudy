## Introduction

~ㄹ/을걸(요) is used to make or refute a statement in doubt. It indicates one's guess or presumption.

## To State or Refute in an Unsure Way: ~ㄹ/을걸(요)

### Responding to a question
- 이 소스가 뭐일까? → 겨자소스일걸 = What is this sauce? → It is probably mustard
- 빨래가 다 말랐나? → 아직 축축할걸 = Is the laundry all dry? → It's probably still a bit wet
- 내가 그 선생님의 수업을 대신하면 별도 수당을 받겠지? → 안 줄걸 = Will I get overtime pay? → They probably won't give it to you

### Disagreeing or refuting a statement
- 우리는 지금 안 가면 늦을 거야 → 안 늦을걸 = We will be late → We probably won't be late
- 그 사람은 한국어를 엄청 잘하네 → 한국어가 모국어일걸 = He's really good at Korean → Korean is probably his mother tongue
- 포도는 건강에 엄청 안 좋은 거야 → 포도당이 많아서 운동하고 먹으면 좋을걸 = Grapes are really not good for you → There is a lot of glucose in grapes, so it is probably good to eat them after exercise

### Past tense: ~았/었을걸
- 배가 출발했나? → 선장님한테 연락이 없어서 아직 출발 안 했을걸 = Did the boat leave? → No word from the captain yet, so it probably hasn't left yet
- 아빠가 돈을 다 썼나 봐 → 조금 남았을걸 = It seems like dad spent all the money → There is probably a little left

### Commonly used with 아니다
- 그 아저씨가 어부일까? → 아닐걸 = Do you think he is a fisherman? → Probably not
- 은박지에 고기를 구워도 몸에 나쁘지 않겠지? → 아닐걸 = Can't be bad for health, right? → It's probably not good
